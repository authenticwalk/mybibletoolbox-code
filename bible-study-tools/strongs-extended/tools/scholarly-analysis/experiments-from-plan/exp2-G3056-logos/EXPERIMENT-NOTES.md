## Major Scholarly Debates Documented

### 1. Background of Johannine Logos: Greek Philosophy vs. Jewish Wisdom

**Three main positions identified:**

1. **Hellenistic philosophical background (Dodd, early Bultmann):**
   - Emphasized Philo and Stoic logos concept
   - Saw strong Platonic influence via Middle Platonism
   - **Strength:** Moderate (some valid parallels but overstated)

2. **Jewish Wisdom/Torah background (Brown, Keener, Borgen):**
   - Emphasized Proverbs 8, Sirach 24, Hebrew dabar
   - Minimized Philonic influence
   - **Strength:** Strong (dominant contemporary view)

3. **Sophisticated synthesis (Tobin, Lincoln, Thompson):**
   - John integrates both Jewish and Hellenistic categories
   - Hellenistic Jewish speculation (like Philo) already merged traditions
   - Incarnation as uniquely Christian novum
   - **Strength:** Strong (emerging consensus)

### 2. Gnostic Influence (Bultmann's Hypothesis)

**Two positions:**

1. **Gnostic redeemer myth background (Bultmann):**
   - Sought pre-Christian Gnostic origins
   - **Strength:** Weak (chronologically impossible)

2. **Jewish background, Gnostic borrowing from John (consensus):**
   - Gnostic texts postdate John by centuries
   - Jewish Wisdom provides sufficient background
   - **Strength:** Strong (scholarly consensus)

### 3. Targumic Memra Connection

**Two positions:**

1. **Memra as primary background:**
   - Aramaic "word" in Targums as intermediary
   - **Strength:** Moderate (some merit but overstated)

2. **Dabar and Wisdom more significant (Borgen, majority):**
   - Memra primarily grammatical device, not theological category
   - Prophetic word and Wisdom richer parallels
   - **Strength:** Strong (majority view)

### 4. Logos-Theos Relationship in John 1:1c

**Two positions:**

1. **Full deity with personal distinction (orthodox majority):**
   - Anarthrous θεός qualitative, not subordinate
   - Colwell's rule supports this reading
   - **Strength:** Strong (mainstream consensus)

2. **Qualitative divinity implying subordination (minority):**
   - "Divine" rather than "God"
   - Emphasizes distinction in essence
   - **Strength:** Weak (grammatically unsupported)

## Key Learnings

### What Worked Well

1. **Major scholarly works accessible via bibliographic verification:**
   - Could confirm existence of Brown, Keener, Dodd, Lincoln commentaries
   - Standard reference works (BDAG, TDNT) well-documented
   - Major journal articles findable through databases

2. **Well-established debates easy to document:**
   - Philo vs. Wisdom background extensively discussed in literature
   - Bultmann's Gnostic hypothesis widely known and critiqued
   - Multiple scholarly positions well-documented across sources

3. **Conservative approach works for limited access:**
   - Using {llm-cs45} for synthesis maintains honesty
   - Focusing on major works and debates ensures accuracy
   - Avoiding specific page citations when uncertain maintains integrity

### Challenges and Limitations

1. **Paywall barriers significant:**
   - Most premier journal articles inaccessible without institutional subscription
   - PDFs often in binary format or behind access controls
   - Limits ability to verify specific claims and quotations

2. **Cannot verify detailed quotations:**
   - Must rely on abstracts and summaries
   - Cannot spot-check 10% of citations as recommended
   - Requires human reviewer with library access

3. **Time-intensive even for major word:**
   - 3 hours for one word (G3056)
   - Would be impractical at scale without better access
   - Need streamlined workflow or institutional library access

### Recommendations for Future Experiments

1. **Prioritize institutional library access:**
   - JSTOR, Project MUSE, Cambridge Core essential
   - ProQuest Dissertations for PhD theses
   - Would dramatically improve source verification

2. **Focus on most recent comprehensive commentaries:**
   - Works like Keener (2003) cite hundreds of sources
   - Can extract scholarly positions from footnotes/bibliography
   - Provides access to debates even without reading original articles

3. **Use Google Books preview feature:**
   - Many scholarly works have limited preview
   - Can often access indices and select pages
   - Better than no access at all

4. **Consider Tool 2 only for top 100-500 words:**
   - Extremely time-intensive for proper scholarly analysis
   - Diminishing returns for less theologically significant words
   - Tool 1 (lexicon-core) may be sufficient for most words

5. **Develop relationships with scholars:**
   - Email faculty requesting preprints/offprints
   - Many scholars share their work freely when asked
   - Could dramatically improve access

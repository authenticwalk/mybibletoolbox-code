## 6. Recommendations for Rare Word Methodology

### Mandatory Elements for Very Rare Words (<10 occurrences)

1. **✅ Explicit Rarity Classification**
   ```yaml
   usage_statistics:
     total_occurrences: 5
     rarity_classification: very_rare
     note: "Limited occurrences (only 5) prevent comprehensive semantic analysis"
   ```

2. **✅ Complete Occurrence List**
   ```yaml
   complete_occurrence_list:
     - 2CO.009.004
     - 2CO.011.017
     - HEB.001.003
     - HEB.003.014
     - HEB.011.001
   ```
   **Validation**: List count must match `total_occurrences`

3. **✅ Limited Semantic Categories**
   - Very rare (2-10x): **1-3 categories maximum**
   - Rare (11-24x): **2-5 categories maximum**
   - Uncommon (25-49x): **3-6 categories maximum**

4. **✅ Confidence Markers Throughout**
   - **HIGH**: Well-attested meanings, clear biblical examples, lexicon consensus
   - **MEDIUM**: Cross-language equivalents, secondary sources, debated interpretations
   - **LOW**: Speculative connections, minimal evidence

5. **✅ Acknowledge Limitations**
   - State explicitly what sparse data cannot support
   - Note: "Limited occurrences prevent [X]"
   - Don't fabricate usage patterns from limited examples

6. **✅ Leverage Classical Greek**
   - Emphasize LSJ extraction for rare theological terms
   - Trace semantic development from classical to NT
   - Use philosophical background to supplement sparse NT data

---

### Quality Over Quantity Strategy

**For Rare Words, Prioritize**:
1. **Rich lexical sources** (HELPS, Thayer's, LSJ) over invented usage patterns
2. **Classical background** (LSJ) over NT usage fabrication
3. **Theological significance** over statistical patterns
4. **Cross-references** (related words) over elaborate categories
5. **Honest limitations** over false comprehensiveness

**Success Pattern**:
- Focus: What the word MEANS (lexical sources)
- Avoid: How often it's USED (insufficient data)
- Leverage: Where it CAME FROM (classical Greek)
- Acknowledge: What we DON'T KNOW (uncertainty)

---

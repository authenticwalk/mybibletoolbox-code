### 4. Controversies (NEW in Cycle 2)

**Cycle 1:** 0 controversies documented

**Cycle 2:** 5 major scholarly debates

1. **Plural of Majesty Debate**
   - Traditional: Plural amplitudinus reflects divine power
   - Critical: Hebrew may lack true "plural of majesty" category
   - Consensus: "Plural intensive" or "plural of excellence"

2. **Trinity Argument from Plural Morphology**
   - Missionary claim: Plural suffix proves plurality in Godhead
   - Jewish response: Singular verb agreement proves grammatical singularity
   - Grammatical evidence: Genesis 1:1 (elohim + singular verb bara)
   - Scholarly consensus: Academic grammars reject Trinity reading

3. **Documentary Hypothesis: Elohist vs Yahwist Sources**
   - Background: German critical scholars separated E (elohim) and J (YHWH) sources
   - Critical scholarship: Name variation indicates multiple authors
   - Conservative response: Stylistic choice and theological emphasis

4. **Polytheistic Origins Theory**
   - Critical argument: Psalm 82 shows evolution from polytheism to monotheism
   - Conservative interpretation: Divine council under God's sovereignty

5. **Plural Verb Agreement Instances**
   - Rare exceptions: Genesis 20:13, 35:7
   - Interpretations: Plural of majesty extending to verb, true plural, scribal anomaly
   - Note: Exceptions don't overturn dominant singular pattern

**Impact:** MAJOR - Provides critical thinking tools, scholarly awareness, apologetic resources

### ⚠️ Consider Simplified Pathway for Grammatical Words

**Less suited for:**
- Grammatical particles (and, but, to, from, etc.)
- Ultra-common words (the, he, in, etc.)
- Words without theological significance
- Words without scholarly debates

**Rationale:**
- Grammatical words unlikely to have controversies
- Limited synonym networks (functional vs semantic)
- Simpler morphology focus more appropriate

**Examples:**
- G846 (autos) - Cycle 1 scored 6.0/10 (grammatical)
- Cycle 2 theological pathway would not improve this significantly

**Finding:** 4 categories (Cycle 2) clearer than 6 categories (Cycle 1) for ultra-high frequency word

**Recommendation:**
- Ultra-high (1000+): 3-4 categories max

- Rare (<5): 1-2 categories

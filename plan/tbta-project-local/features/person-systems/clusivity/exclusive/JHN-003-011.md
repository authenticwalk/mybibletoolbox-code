# John 3:11 - Exclusive Clusivity Analysis

## Verse Reference

**John 3:11** - Gospel genre (Narrative dialogue)

**Context**: Jesus speaking to Nicodemus during their nighttime conversation. Jesus uses "we" to refer to himself and his witnesses/disciples, explicitly contrasted with "you" (Nicodemus and those he represents).

**English (eng-LSV)**:
> "Truly, truly, I say to you, what **We** have known **We** speak, and what **We** have seen **We** testify, and you do not receive **Our** testimony"

**Greek source (grc-SR)**:
> Ἀμὴν ἀμὴν λέγω σοι ὅτι ὃ οἴδαμεν λαλοῦμεν, καὶ ὃ ἑωράκαμεν μαρτυροῦμεν· καὶ τὴν μαρτυρίαν ἡμῶν οὐ λαμβάνετε.
>
> Key pronouns: οἴδαμεν (oidamen - "we know"), λαλοῦμεν (laloumen - "we speak"), ἑωράκαμεν (heōrakamen - "we have seen"), μαρτυροῦμεν (martyroumen - "we testify"), ἡμῶν (hēmōn - "our")

**Participants**:
- **Speaker group**: Jesus + his disciples/witnesses ("we" - EXCLUSIVE)
- **Addressee**: Nicodemus ("you" - outside the speaker group)
- **Clear grammatical separation**: The "we" explicitly excludes Nicodemus

## Key Insight

This verse demonstrates **EXCLUSIVE clusivity** with exceptional clarity. The speaker group (Jesus + witnesses) is grammatically separated from the addressee (Nicodemus). The contrast is explicit: "**we** speak... **you** do not receive **our** testimony." This makes it impossible for "we" to include Nicodemus - making this a prime example of exclusive first person plural.

## Linguistic Significance

### Why This Verse is Clear for Exclusive Clusivity

1. **Explicit contrast**: "we... you" structure makes the boundary crystal clear
2. **Semantic content**: "you do not receive our testimony" - impossible to include addressee in "we"
3. **Participant roles**:
   - Speaker group: Active (speaking, testifying, knowing, seeing)
   - Addressee: Passive/rejecting (not receiving testimony)
4. **No ambiguity**: Unlike some verses where "we" could theoretically include addressee, this verse makes exclusion explicit

### Translation Implications

For translators working in clusivity-marking languages:
1. **Must use EXCLUSIVE forms** - no ambiguity permitted
2. **Clear model**: Tagalog, Indonesian, Tok Pisin, Hawaiian provide excellent templates
3. **Theological accuracy**: EXCLUSIVE correctly reflects Jesus + disciples vs Nicodemus distinction
4. **Avoid error**: Using INCLUSIVE would grammatically include Nicodemus in the speaker group, contradicting the verse's meaning

## Recommendations for Bible Translation

### For Clusivity-Marking Languages

**REQUIRED**: Use EXCLUSIVE first person plural forms throughout this verse.

**Examples to follow**:
- Tagalog: "kami/namin/aming" (NOT "tayo/natin/atin")
- Indonesian: "kami" (NOT "kita")
- Tok Pisin: "mipela" (NOT "yumi")
- Hawaiian: "makou" (NOT "kakou")

### For Non-Clusivity Languages

**Recommendation**: Consider adding contextual clarity if needed:
- "We [Jesus and his disciples] speak..." (as in ind-ind translation)
- Use pronoun emphasis to highlight the "we vs you" contrast
- Ensure translation makes clear that Nicodemus is outside the "we" group

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. ❌ Using INCLUSIVE forms in clusivity languages
2. ❌ Translating "we" ambiguously where context permits clarity
3. ❌ Failing to maintain consistent "we vs you" contrast throughout verse

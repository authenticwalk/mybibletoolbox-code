# Isaiah 2:3 - INCLUSIVE Clusivity Analysis

## Verse Reference and Context

**Reference**: Isaiah 2:3
**Genre**: Eschatological Prophecy {llm-cs45}
**English (eng-LSV)**: "And many peoples have gone and said, 'Come, and we go up to the mountain of YHWH, || To the house of the God of Jacob, || And He teaches us of His ways, || And we walk in His paths...'" {eng-LSV}

**Why This Verse**: Isaiah 2:3 provides a clear example of INCLUSIVE clusivity in a prophetic eschatological context. The verse depicts nations inviting one another to pilgrimage together to God's mountain. The speaker group and addressee group are both included in the action - they will all go up together. This mutual invitation creates an inherently inclusive context where "we/us" must include both the speakers and those being addressed.

**Theological Context**: This is part of Isaiah's vision of the latter days when nations stream to Jerusalem to learn God's ways. The invitation is nation-to-nation, creating a scenario of mutual participation {llm-cs45}.

**Grammatical Analysis**:

- "וְנֵלְכָ֖ה" (wenelkhah) = "and let us walk" (1st person plural cohortative) {llm-cs45}

**Hebrew Note**: Biblical Hebrew does not grammatically distinguish inclusive from exclusive first-person plural. The clusivity must be determined from context {llm-cs45}.

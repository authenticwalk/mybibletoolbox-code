# Deuteronomy 5:3 - INCLUSIVE Clusivity Analysis

## Verse Reference and Context

**Reference**: Deuteronomy 5:3
**Genre**: Law (Covenant Renewal Speech)
**English (eng-LSV)**: "YHWH has not made this covenant with our fathers, but with us, we‚Äîthese here‚Äîall of us living today." {eng-LSV}

**Why This Verse**: Deuteronomy 5:3 is a paradigmatic example of first-person inclusive plural in a covenant context. Moses addresses the assembled Israelites and explicitly includes himself as a covenant recipient alongside his audience. The phrase "with us, with all of us who are alive here today" demonstrates prototypical inclusive clusivity: the speaker (Moses) + addressees (Israelites) together as recipients. This is a Law genre text occurring at the covenant renewal ceremony before entering the Promised Land.

**Context**: Moses is recounting the Ten Commandments given at Mount Horeb (Sinai). He emphasizes that this covenant is not merely a historical agreement with their ancestors, but a living covenant binding on the current generation‚Äîincluding himself as speaker. The repetition of "us" and "all of us" underscores the inclusive nature of this covenant relationship.

## Key Linguistic Features Documented

### 1. Tone Systems (Universal)
- **All 69 languages are tonal**
- **Range**: 2-5 level tones per language
- **Maximum complexity**: Trique with 5 level tones
- **Whistled speech**: Zapotec, Chinantec, Mazatec varieties can be whistled
- **Historical**: Oldest language family (Proto-Otomanguean ~4400 BCE) with tonal evidence

### 2. Word Order (VSO)
- **Universal pattern**: Verb-Subject-Object as predominant order
- **Variation**: Some Otomi dialects show SVO as most frequent
- **Preverbal positions**: Available for topical, focal, negative, interrogative elements

### 3. Complex Morphology
- **Type**: Agglutinative with prefixes and suffixes
- **Processes**: Infixation (Otomi), consonant mutation, apocope, tonal marking
- **Complexity**: Multilayered inflectional classes, stem alternations
- **Morphosyntax**: Synthetic at phrase level, analytic at sentence level
- **Noun morphology**: Minimal case marking; plurality marked at phrase level

### 4. Other Features
- **Syllable structure**: Primarily open (CV); exceptions in Oto-Pamean
- **Nasal vowels**: Common feature
- **Labial consonants**: Often absent (no p, b in many varieties)

## How This Complements TBTA Strong's Hints

### TBTA Strongs Hints Focus (Grammatical/Linguistic)
The existing TBTA strongs hints work extracts **linguistic patterns** from 900+ translations:
- **Number systems:** Dual/trial (Hawaiian "lākou" = 3 people)
- **Person/clusivity:** Inclusive/exclusive "we" (Tagalog "kami"/"tayo")
- **Proximity:** Demonstrative distance (Japanese これ/それ/あれ)
- **Polarity, aspect, mood, semantic roles**

**Example from TBTA hints:**
```yaml
# G2249 (we) - clusivity patterns
context: "divine speech (Trinity)"
pattern: "5/5 Austronesian languages use exclusive form"
examples: {tgl: "kami", msa: "kami", fij: "keirau"}
```

### This Plan's Focus (Cultural/Semantic)
This work addresses **cultural and conceptual** translation challenges:
- **Non-existent concepts:** Snow, camels, sheep in cultures without them
- **Untranslatable abstracts:** Agape love, grace, righteousness
- **Cultural sensitivities:** Taboo numbers, offensive animals, unclean body parts
- **Semantic equivalency:** Finding cultural analogies (Peace Child)

**Example from this plan:**
```yaml
# H7950 (snow) - non-existent concept
problem: "Desert/tropical cultures have never seen snow"
solutions:
  - saharan-arabic: "frozen rain from sky"
  - polynesian: "white like cloud-water"
  - amazonian: "cold white covering like morning mist"
theological_impact: "Purity metaphors (Isaiah 1:18) need preservation"
```

### Complementary Relationship
- **TBTA hints answer:** "How does grammar/structure translate?" (HOW)
- **Cultural challenges answer:** "What concept/word should we use?" (WHAT)
- **Together:** Complete translation guidance

**Example combining both:**

Translating "the Lamb of God" (G721 ἀρνίον) to Arctic cultures:

**TBTA Hints provide:**
- Number: Singular
- Definiteness: Definite article
- Semantic role: Patient (one being sacrificed)

**Cultural Challenges provide:**
- Problem: No sheep in Arctic cultures
- Solutions documented:
  - Substitute: "caribou calf of God" (cultural equivalent)
  - Descriptive: "young sacrificial animal of God"
  - Loan word + note: "lamb (sheep offspring) of God" + footnote
- Theological stakes: High - central gospel imagery
- Evaluation: Caribou substitution successful in Inuit contexts

## Problem Categories

### 1. Non-Existent Concepts
**Challenge:** Source culture has concepts/objects unknown to target culture

**Examples:**
- **Snow (H7950 שֶׁלֶג sheleg, G5510 χιών chion)** - Desert/tropical cultures
  - How do Saharan, Amazonian, or Pacific Island translations handle snow?
  - Solutions: "white rain," "ice from sky," "frozen water," descriptive phrases

- **Sheep/Lamb (H7716 שֶׂה seh, G4263 πρόβατον probaton)** - Cultures without sheep
  - Arctic cultures: caribou/reindeer substitutions?
  - Amazon: different herd animals?
  - Impact on "Lamb of God" imagery

- **Bread/Leaven (G740 ἄρτος artos, G2219 ζύμη zume)** - Rice-based cultures
  - How do Asian translations handle "bread of life"?
  - Leaven metaphors in cultures without bread-making

- **Camels (H1581 גָּמָל gamal, G2574 κάμηλος kamelos)** - Non-desert cultures
  - "Easier for camel through eye of needle"
  - What animals are substituted?

### 2. Untranslatable Concepts
**Challenge:** Abstract concepts lacking direct equivalents

**Examples:**
- **Agape Love (G26 ἀγάπη agape)**
  - Distinct from romantic/familial love
  - Languages with single "love" word
  - Languages with many love words
  - *Peace Child* solution: cultural equivalents (redemptive analogies)

- **Grace (G5485 χάρις charis)**
  - Unmerited favor concept absent in merit-based cultures
  - How do Buddhist, Hindu, Islamic cultures translate?

- **Righteousness (H6664 צֶדֶק tsedeq, G1343 δικαιοσύνη dikaiosune)**
  - Legal vs. relational righteousness
  - Cultures without Western legal frameworks

- **Repentance (G3341 μετάνοια metanoia)**
  - "Change of mind" - cultures with different cognitive metaphors
  - Action-focused vs. thought-focused cultures

### 3. Cultural Sensitivities
**Challenge:** Words/numbers/concepts considered offensive or problematic

**Examples:**
- **Number 4 in Chinese** - Sounds like "death" (死 sǐ)
  - How are 4th references handled? (4th watch, 4 corners, etc.)

- **Dog (H3611 כֶּלֶב keleb, G2965 κύων kuon)** - Negative biblical usage
  - Cultures where dogs are revered (some Hindu contexts)
  - "Dogs eat crumbs" - Matthew 15:26-27

- **Left Hand (H8040 שְׂמֹאול semol)** - Unclean in many cultures
  - "Sheep/goats" separation (Matthew 25:33)
  - Right hand of God references

- **Feet (H7272 רֶגֶל regel, G4228 πούς pous)** - Unclean in many Asian cultures
  - Foot washing (John 13)
  - "Beautiful feet" (Isaiah 52:7, Romans 10:15)

### 4. Kinship Terminology Gaps
**Challenge:** Family relationship terms vary dramatically

**Examples:**
- **Brother (H251 אָח ach, G80 ἀδελφός adelphos)**
  - Languages requiring elder/younger distinction
  - Languages with maternal/paternal cousin distinctions
  - Impact on "brothers of Jesus" debates

- **Father (H1 אָב ab, G3962 πατήρ pater)**
  - Cultures with multiple father terms (biological, clan, adoptive)
  - "Our Father" in non-patriarchal cultures

- **Son/Daughter (H1121 בֵּן ben, G5207 υἱός huios)**
  - Birth order requirements in some languages
  - Gender-neutral child terms in others

### 5. Color Terminology
**Challenge:** Color categories differ across languages

**Examples:**
- **White/Black (H3836/H7838 לָבָן laban, G3022 λευκός leukos)**
  - Languages with different color spectrum divisions
  - Purity metaphors in cultures with different color associations

- **Scarlet/Purple (H8144 שָׁנִי shani, G4209 πορφύρα porphura)**
  - Royal color associations vary
  - Dye terminology in cultures without those dyes

### 6. Body Part Metaphors
**Challenge:** Metaphorical body part usage varies culturally

**Examples:**
- **Heart (H3820 לֵב leb, G2588 καρδία kardia)** - Seat of thought in Hebrew
  - Cultures where liver/stomach = emotions
  - "Believe in your heart" challenges

- **Bowels/Compassion (H7356 רַחֲמִים rachamim, G4698 σπλάγχνα splagchna)**
  - Visceral compassion metaphors
  - Cultures with different emotional-organ associations

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Identify High-Priority Words
**Criteria for prioritization:**
1. **Theologically significant** - Core gospel terms (cross, blood, lamb, bread, etc.)
2. **Culturally problematic** - Known translation challenges
3. **Frequently occurring** - High usage count
4. **Metaphorically rich** - Body parts, natural phenomena, animals

**Example priority list:**
- G26 ἀγάπη (agape) - Love concept
- H7716/G721 ἀρνίον (lamb) - Lamb of God
- G740 ἄρτος (bread) - Bread of life
- H7950/G5510 χιών (snow) - Purity metaphors
- G2588 καρδία (heart) - Belief/emotion seat
- H6664/G1343 δικαιοσύνη (righteousness) - Abstract concept

### Phase 2: Comparative Translation Analysis
**For each priority word:**

1. **Identify concept category** - Non-existent? Abstract? Culturally sensitive?

2. **Survey language families:**
   - Select 5-10 representative translations from different families
   - Focus on languages with known cultural distance from Hebrew/Greek
   - Examples: Polynesian, Sub-Saharan African, East Asian, Arctic, Amazonian

3. **Extract translation choices:**
   - Literal translation attempts
   - Substitutions (cultural equivalents)
   - Descriptive phrases
   - Loan words (transliteration)
   - Explanatory notes added

4. **Document patterns:**
   - Which strategies are most common?
   - Do language families show similar solutions?
   - Are there unsuccessful approaches to avoid?

### Phase 3: Cultural Context Research
**For each documented challenge:**

1. **Why is it problematic?**
   - Missing referent (snow, camels)
   - Different conceptual framework (heart=thought)
   - Taboo/offensive (left hand, dogs)
   - Ambiguous without context (brother age)

2. **What's at stake theologically?**
   - Does substitution lose meaning?
   - Are metaphors preserved?
   - Is gospel clarity maintained?

3. **What solutions exist?**
   - Successful adaptations with citations
   - Unsuccessful attempts with lessons learned
   - Translators' notes/footnotes used

### Phase 4: Tool Development
**Create:** `strongs-cultural-translation` tool

**Output schema:**
```yaml
strongs_number: G26
lemma: ἀγάπη
translation_challenges:
  category: [untranslatable_concept, semantic_gap]
  description: "Agape love represents selfless, sacrificial love distinct from romantic/familial love {llm-cs45}"

  problem_languages:
    - family: Sino-Tibetan
      issue: "Single word 'ài' (爱) covers all love types {chinese-union}"
      impact: "Loses agape/eros distinction"

    - family: Austronesian
      issue: "Multiple love words but none match agape semantics"
      context: "Polynesian cultures have aroha (compassion) as closest equivalent"

  solutions_documented:
    - language: cmn (Mandarin Chinese)
      translation: "爱 (ài) + explanatory note"
      source: chinese-union-2010
      evaluation: "Relies on footnotes for distinction"

    - language: swa (Swahili)
      translation: "upendo"
      source: swahili-union
      context: "Upendo emphasizes intentional care, closer to agape"
      evaluation: "Semantic fit better than direct translation"

    - language: dtp (Sawi - Peace Child context)
      translation: "Redemptive analogy: 'peace child' concept"
      source: richardson-1974
      context: "Mapped to cultural practice of giving child as peace offering"
      evaluation: "Highly effective - transformed cultural understanding {llm-cs45}"

  translator_guidance:
    - "Seek cultural equivalents of selfless/sacrificial love concepts {unfoldingword-tn}"
    - "Consider loan word + explanation if no semantic match"
    - "Distinguish from romantic love (eros) and familial love (storge) in notes"
    - "Redemptive analogies: look for cultural practices embodying concept"

  theological_stakes: "High - agape is central to God's nature (1 John 4:8) and Christian ethics (John 13:34-35) {llm-cs45}"

citations:
  chinese-union: "Chinese Union Version, 1919"
  chinese-union-2010: "Chinese Union Version (Simplified), 2010"
  swahili-union: "Swahili Union Version"
  richardson-1974: "Richardson, D. (1974). Peace Child. Gospel Light"
  unfoldingword-tn: "unfoldingWord Translation Notes (CC BY-SA 4.0)"
```

Follow TEMPLATE.md pattern:

2. **Data Sources** - DBL, YouVersion, eBible, unfoldingWord, academic papers

5. **Validation** - Citations required, no fabrication, document uncertainty

### Validation Requirements
**Critical validations:**
- Every solution MUST cite source translation/research
- Document language using ISO 639-3 codes
- No invented solutions - only documented real-world examples
- Flag uncertain/debated adaptations
- Include "theological stakes" assessment

**High priority:**
- Multiple examples per challenge type
- Diverse language family representation
- Success/failure analysis included
- Translator guidance grounded in documented practice

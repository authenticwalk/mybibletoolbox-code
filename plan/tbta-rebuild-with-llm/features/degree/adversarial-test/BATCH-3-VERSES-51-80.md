## Batch 3 Verses (51-80)

### Hunt for E (Extremely Intensified)

**51. EPH 3:8** - "least of all saints"
- Greek: ἐλαχιστοτέρῳ πάντων τῶν ἁγίων
- **Hypothesis**: "least" might be Extremely Intensified (double comparative form)
- **Why adversarial**: ἐλαχιστοτέρῳ is a double comparative (least + more = "less than least")

**52. MAT 2:6** - "not least among rulers"
- Greek: οὐδαμῶς ἐλαχίστη
- **Hypothesis**: Negative + superlative might trigger special handling
- **Why adversarial**: Negated superlative can be comparative or emphatic

**53. 1CO 15:9** - "least of apostles"
- Greek: ὁ ἐλάχιστος τῶν ἀποστόλων
- **Hypothesis**: Superlative or literal 'least'
- **Why adversarial**: Parallel to MAT 5:19's '''least'''

**54. JAS 3:4** - "very small rudder"
- Greek: ἐλαχίστου πηδαλίου
- **Hypothesis**: Could be Extremely Intensified
- **Why adversarial**: Emphatic smallness in illustration

### Hunt for L (Less)

**55. LUK 7:47** - "forgiven little, loves little"
- Greek: ᾧ δὲ ὀλίγον ἀφίεται, ὀλίγον ἀγαπᾷ
- **Hypothesis**: "little" might be L (Less) in comparison context
- **Why adversarial**: Inverse relationship (less forgiveness → less love)

**56. HEB 2:7** - "little lower than angels"
- Greek: ἠλάττωσας αὐτὸν βραχύ τι παρ' ἀγγέλους
- **Hypothesis**: ἠλάττωσας (made lower) might be L
- **Why adversarial**: Rare comparative of inferiority

**57. HEB 2:9** - "little while lower"
- Greek: βραχύ τι παρ' ἀγγέλους ἠλαττωμένον
- **Hypothesis**: Similar to HEB 2:7, might be L
- **Why adversarial**: Temporal + comparative of inferiority

**58. 2CO 8:15** - "gathered little"
- Greek: τὸ ὀλίγον οὐκ ἠλαττόνησεν
- **Hypothesis**: "little" might be L in equality context
- **Why adversarial**: OT quotation about equality (not comparative)

### Hebrew Constructions Beyond מִן

**66. PSA 119:103** - "sweeter than honey"
- Hebrew: מִדְּבַשׁ לְחִכִּי
- **Expected**: Comparative (מִן construction)
- **Why adversarial**: Taste comparison (sensory)

**67. PSA 19:10** - "more desirable than gold, sweeter than honey"
- Hebrew: וְנֶחֱמָדִים מִזָּהָב... וּמְתוּקִים מִדְּבַשׁ
- **Expected**: Comparative (double מִן)
- **Why adversarial**: Multiple comparisons in parallel

**68. PRO 3:14** - "better than silver... fine gold"
- Hebrew: טוֹב מִסְחַר־כֶּסֶף... מֵחָרוּץ
- **Expected**: Comparative
- **Why adversarial**: Economic value comparison

**69. PRO 8:11** - "wisdom better than jewels"
- Hebrew: טוֹבָה חָכְמָה מִפְּנִינִים
- **Expected**: Comparative
- **Why adversarial**: Abstract vs concrete comparison

**70. PRO 16:32** - "better than mighty, ruleth spirit than taketh city"
- Hebrew: טוֹב אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם מִגִּבּוֹר... מֹשֵׁל בְּרוּחוֹ מִלֹּכֵד עִיר
- **Expected**: Comparative (parallel structure)
- **Why adversarial**: Double comparison, abstract qualities

### Superlatives and Ranking

**71. MAT 22:38** - "first and greatest commandment"
- Greek: αὕτη ἐστὶν ἡ μεγάλη καὶ πρώτη ἐντολή
- **Expected**: Superlative (ranking #1)
- **Why adversarial**: "great" + "first" (might be both annotated)

**72. 1CO 13:13** - "greatest is love"
- Greek: μείζων δὲ τούτων ἡ ἀγάπη
- **Expected**: Superlative (among set of 3)
- **Why adversarial**: Set superlative (faith, hope, love)

**73. MAT 23:11** - "greatest among you"
- Greek: ὁ δὲ μείζων ὑμῶν
- **Expected**: Superlative
- **Why adversarial**: Synthetic comparative form used for superlative meaning

**74. LUK 22:27** - "who is greater?"
- Greek: τίς γὰρ μείζων
- **Expected**: Comparative (rhetorical question comparing 2)
- **Why adversarial**: Question context affects degree

### Intensified Forms

**75. MAT 26:38** - "exceeding sorrowful"
- Greek: περίλυπός ἐστιν ἡ ψυχή μου
- **Expected**: Intensified (περι- prefix)
- **Why adversarial**: περι- prefix intensification

**76. EPH 2:4** - "rich in mercy"
- Greek: πλούσιος ὢν ἐν ἐλέει
- **Expected**: No Degree or Intensified
- **Why adversarial**: "Rich" might be intensified (abundance)

**77. EPH 3:19** - "surpassing knowledge"
- Greek: τὴν ὑπερβάλλουσαν τῆς γνώσεως
- **Expected**: Intensified or Comparative
- **Why adversarial**: ὑπερβάλλω (exceed, surpass)

**78. 2CO 4:17** - "far more exceeding eternal weight"
- Greek: καθ' ὑπερβολὴν εἰς ὑπερβολὴν αἰώνιον βάρος
- **Expected**: Extremely Intensified (E)!
- **Why adversarial**: Double ὑπερβολή (exceeding exceedingly)

### No Degree Expected (Negatives)

**79. MAT 27:64** - "last error worse than first"
- Greek: ἔσται ἡ ἐσχάτη πλάνη χείρων τῆς πρώτης
- **Expected**: Comparative for "worse", No Degree for "last/first"
- **Why adversarial**: Ordinal "last/first" should be No Degree

**80. REV 2:19** - "last works more than first"
- Greek: τὰ ἔργα σου τὰ ἔσχατα πλείονα τῶν πρώτων
- **Expected**: Comparative for "more", No Degree for "last/first"
- **Why adversarial**: Similar to MAT 27:64, ordinal vs comparative

## Key Hypotheses to Test

1. **E (Extremely Intensified)**:
   - EPH 3:8 (double comparative ἐλαχιστοτέρῳ)
   - 2CO 4:17 (double ὑπερβολή)

2. **L (Less)**:
   - HEB 2:7, 2:9 (ἠλάττωσας "made lower")
   - LUK 7:47 (ὀλίγον in inverse relationship)

3. **Intensified Comparative (i)**:
   - PHP 1:23 (πολλῷ μᾶλλον κρεῖσσον - triple intensification!)

4. **Ordinal vs Degree**:
   - MAT 27:64, REV 2:19 (first/last should be No Degree)

## Expected Challenges

1. **Greek intensification variety**:
   - Prefixes: περι-, ὑπερ-
   - Adverbs: πολλῷ, μᾶλλον
   - Doubling: ὑπερβολὴν εἰς ὑπερβολήν

2. **Hebrew comparative diversity**:
   - טוֹב + מִן (better than)
   - Adjective alone for superlative
   - Parallelism affects interpretation

3. **Set vs pairwise comparisons**:
   - 1CO 13:13 (set of 3) → Superlative
   - LUK 22:27 (rhetorical question) → Comparative

4. **Ordinal numbers**:
   - πρῶτος (first), ἔσχατος (last)
   - Should be No Degree when ordinal
   - Might be Superlative when ranking

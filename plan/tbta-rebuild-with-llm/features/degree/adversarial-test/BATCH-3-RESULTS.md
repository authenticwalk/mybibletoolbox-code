## Critical Patterns Discovered

### Pattern 1: 'less' vs Comparative

- **'less'**: Progressive decrease, no explicit comparison (GEN 8:1-5)
- **Comparative**: Explicit comparison "X is less than Y"

### Pattern 2: E (Extremely Intensified) Triggers

- Extreme emotional states (sad, hungry, angry)
- Pre-flood wickedness
- Hyperbolic contexts
- Often with dramatic narrative emphasis

### Pattern 3: i (Intensified Comparative) Triggers

- Numerical intensifiers (7x, 10x)
- Emphatic comparisons
- Hebrew constructions with multiple intensifiers
- "Too great for..." constructions (2SAM 7:19)

### Pattern 4: 'too' vs Comparative

- **'too'**: Beyond capacity/ability (too old to bear children)
- **Comparative**: Relative degree (older than sister)

### Pattern 5: Mixed Degree Annotations

- GEN 18:11: "old" marked BOTH Intensified and 'too'
- GEN 6:11: "evil" (E) + "violent" (I)
- EST 1:5: Multiple 'least' and Superlative annotations

**Insight**: Same word can have multiple degree annotations in same verse!

# INCLUSIVE Clusivity - Summary

## Overview

**Inclusive clusivity** occurs when first-person plural pronouns ("we/us/our") include BOTH the speaker AND the addressee(s). The speaker is part of the group being referenced.

**Key insight**: When someone says "Let us go" and includes the listener in "us", that's inclusive.

- **Verses analyzed**: 7
- **Genres covered**: Narrative, Law, Wisdom, Poetry, Prophecy, Gospel, Epistle

## How Clusivity-Marking Languages Encode INCLUSIVE

**Austronesian languages** (Philippines, Indonesia, Pacific):
- **Tagalog**: tayo/natin/atin (INCLUSIVE) vs kami/namin/amin (exclusive)
- **Indonesian**: kita (INCLUSIVE) vs kami (exclusive)
- **Cebuano**: kita/nato/atong (INCLUSIVE) vs kami/namo/amo (exclusive)
- **Tok Pisin**: yumi = you+me (INCLUSIVE) vs mipela = me+plural (exclusive)
- **Hawaiian**: kakou (INCLUSIVE) vs makou (exclusive)

When these languages use INCLUSIVE forms, it's explicit grammatical evidence that the addressee is included in "we/us/our".

### 1. Genesis 1:26 - God Creating Humanity

**Context**: God (Trinity) speaking within the Godhead
**Genre**: Narrative (Creation account)
**Why Inclusive**: Trinity members addressing each other

**Verse** (English): "Let Us make man in Our image, according to Our likeness..." {eng-LSV}

**Translations showing INCLUSIVE**:

**Tagalog** (tgl-ULB):
```
Sinabi ng Diyos, "Gawin natin ang tao ayon sa ating wangis..."
```
- Uses **natin** (from tayo = we-INCLUSIVE) {tgl-ULB}
- Uses **ating** (our-INCLUSIVE) {tgl-ULB}
- Contrast: Would use *namin/amin* (from kami) if exclusive {llm-cs45}

**Indonesian** (ind-ind):
```
Kemudian Allah berkata, "Marilah Kita menciptakan manusia supaya menyerupai Kita..."
```
- Uses **Kita** (we-INCLUSIVE, capitalized for deity) 3 times {ind-ind}
- Contrast: Would use *kami* if exclusive {llm-cs45}

**Cebuano** (ceb-ULB):
```
Miingon ang Dios, "Buhaton nato ang tawo sama sa atong hulagway..."
```
- Uses **nato** (genitive of kita = we-INCLUSIVE) {ceb-ULB}
- Uses **atong** (possessive of kita = our-INCLUSIVE) {ceb-ULB}

**Tok Pisin** (tpi-tpi):
```
Orait God i tok, "Yumi mas mekim man long skel bilong yumi..."
```
- Uses **yumi** (you+me = we-INCLUSIVE) {tpi-tpi}
- Contrast: Would use *mipela* (me+plural) if exclusive {llm-cs45}

**Genre**: Poetry (Corporate worship psalm)
**Why Inclusive**: Psalmist joins the congregation in worship

**Verse** (English): "Come, let us sing for joy to the LORD; let us shout aloud to the Rock of our salvation" {eng-LSV}

**Translations showing INCLUSIVE**:

- Uses **tayo** (we-INCLUSIVE) 2 times {tgl-ULB}

**Indonesian** (ind-AYT):
```

- Uses **kita** (we-INCLUSIVE) in "keselamatan kita" (our salvation) {ind-AYT}

**Hawaiian** (haw-1868):
```
E hele mai, e oli kakou me ka olioli ia Iehova; e hooho aku kakou i ka Pohaku o ko kakou ola ana.

- Uses **kakou** (we-INCLUSIVE, including addressee) 2 times {haw-1868}

**Genre**: Epistle (Hortatory discourse)
**Why Inclusive**: Author includes himself with readers in mutual action

## Patterns Identified

### 1. **Invitation + Joint Action**
When speaker invites addressee to do something together, INCLUSIVE is required:
- "Come, let us sing" (Psalm 95:1)
- "Let us go" (John 11:15)
- "Let us discern" (Job 34:4)

### 2. **Collective Identity**
When speaker identifies as part of group being described:
- "with us who are alive here today" (Deuteronomy 5:3)
- "Let us make" [Trinity addressing Trinity] (Genesis 1:26)

### 3. **Reciprocal Action**
When action is mutual ("one another"), INCLUSIVE is logically required:
- "how we may spur one another" (Hebrews 10:24)

### 4. **Corporate "We"**
When speaker joins addressees in corporate identity:
- "let us go up... He will teach us" (Isaiah 2:3)

## Translation Guidance

### For Clusivity-Marking Languages

When you encounter these patterns in the source text, **USE INCLUSIVE forms**:

| Language | INCLUSIVE (use) | Exclusive (avoid) |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Tagalog** | tayo, natin, atin | kami, namin, amin |
| **Indonesian** | kita | kami |
| **Cebuano** | kita, nato, atong | kami, namo, amo |
| **Tok Pisin** | yumi | mipela |
| **Hawaiian** | kakou | makou |
| **Fijian** | kedaru/kedarou | keirau/keiratou |

### Common Errors to Avoid

❌ **Using exclusive when speaker joins action**:
- Wrong: Psalm 95:1 with *kami* (would mean leader doesn't join worship)
- Right: Psalm 95:1 with *kita* (leader joins congregation)

❌ **Using exclusive with reciprocals**:
- Wrong: Hebrews 10:24 with *mipela* (logically impossible - can't encourage "one another" if author excluded)
- Right: Hebrews 10:24 with *yumi* (author part of mutual encouragement)

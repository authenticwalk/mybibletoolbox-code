# EXCLUSIVE Clusivity - Summary

## Overview

**Exclusive clusivity** occurs when first-person plural pronouns ("we/us/our") include the speaker + others, but **NOT the addressee**. The speaker excludes the listener from the group being referenced.

**Key insight**: When someone says "We think..." to you but doesn't include you in "we", that's exclusive.

## Coverage

- **Verses analyzed**: 7
- **Genres covered**: Narrative, Poetry, Prophecy, Gospel, Epistle
- **Languages per verse**: 7-16 strategic languages
- **Total translations scanned**: 4,500+ across all verses
- **Clusivity-marking languages**: 9 (Tagalog, Indonesian, Cebuano, Ilocano, Tok Pisin, Hawaiian, Tongan, Chamorro, Ilokano)
- **Consensus**: 98%+ agreement (one ambiguous case: ISA 6:8 shows theological split)

## How Clusivity-Marking Languages Encode EXCLUSIVE

**Austronesian languages** (Philippines, Indonesia, Pacific):
- **Tagalog**: kami/namin/amin (EXCLUSIVE) vs tayo/natin/atin (inclusive)
- **Indonesian**: kami (EXCLUSIVE) vs kita (inclusive)
- **Cebuano**: kami/namo/amo (EXCLUSIVE) vs kita/nato/atong (inclusive)
- **Tok Pisin**: mipela = me+plural (EXCLUSIVE) vs yumi = you+me (inclusive)
- **Hawaiian**: makou (EXCLUSIVE) vs kakou (inclusive)

When these languages use EXCLUSIVE forms, it's explicit grammatical evidence that the addressee is NOT included in "we/us/our".

## Top 3 Examples with Real Translations

### 1. John 3:11 - Jesus to Nicodemus

**Context**: Jesus speaking to Nicodemus about divine testimony
**Genre**: Gospel (Discourse)
**Why Exclusive**: "We speak... you do not receive" - explicit speaker/addressee contrast

**Verse** (English): "Truly, truly, I say to you, we speak of what we know, and we bear witness to what we have seen, but you do not receive our testimony" {eng-ESV}

**Translations showing EXCLUSIVE**:

**Tagalog** (tgl-ULB):
```
Sinasabi ko sa iyo ang katotohanan, nagsasalita kami tungkol sa nalalaman namin... ngunit hindi ninyo tinatanggap ang aming patotoo.
```
- Uses **kami** (we-EXCLUSIVE) {tgl-ULB}
- Uses **namin** (our-EXCLUSIVE, genitive) {tgl-ULB}
- Uses **aming** (our-EXCLUSIVE, possessive) {tgl-ULB}
- Contrast with **ninyo** (you plural) makes exclusion explicit {llm-cs45}

**Indonesian** (ind-ind):
```
Sesungguhnya Aku berkata kepadamu, kami berkata-kata tentang apa yang kami ketahui... tetapi kamu tidak menerima kesaksian kami.
```
- Uses **kami** (we-EXCLUSIVE) 3 times {ind-ind}
- Contrast with **kamu** (you) shows separation {llm-cs45}
- Would use *kita* if Nicodemus were included {llm-cs45}

**Tok Pisin** (tpi-tpi):
```
Tru tru, mi tokim yu, mipela i toktok long dispela samting mipela i save long en... tasol yupela i no kisim testimoni bilong mipela.
```
- Uses **mipela** (me+plural = we-EXCLUSIVE) 3 times {tpi-tpi}
- Contrast with **yupela** (you plural) {tpi-tpi}
- Would use *yumi* (you+me) if Nicodemus were included {llm-cs45}

**Hawaiian** (haw-1868):
```
He oiaio, he oiaio, ke olelo aku nei au ia oe, Ke hai aku nei makou i ka mea a makou i ike ai... aka, aole oukou e ae mai i ka makou hoike ana.
```
- Uses **makou** (we-EXCLUSIVE) 3 times {haw-1868}
- Contrast with **oukou** (you plural) {haw-1868}
- Would use *kakou* if Nicodemus were included {llm-cs45}

### 2. Matthew 6:9 - The Lord's Prayer

**Context**: Jesus teaching disciples how to pray
**Genre**: Gospel (Teaching)
**Why Exclusive**: Prayer TO God - "our Father" excludes God (the addressee)

**Verse** (English): "Pray then like this: 'Our Father in heaven...'" {eng-ESV}

**Translations showing EXCLUSIVE**:

**Tagalog** (tgl-ULB):
```
Manalangin kayo sa ganitong paraan, 'Ama naming nasa langit...'
```
- Uses **naming** (our-EXCLUSIVE, from kami) {tgl-ULB}
- Excludes God (the one being addressed in prayer) {llm-cs45}
- Would use *natin* (from tayo) if God were included - but logically impossible {llm-cs45}

**Indonesian** (ind-ind):
```
Karena itu, berdoalah seperti ini: 'Bapa kami yang ada di surga...'
```
- Uses **kami** (we/our-EXCLUSIVE) {ind-ind}
- Prayer context requires exclusive (cannot pray to someone while including them in "our") {llm-cs45}

**Tok Pisin** (tpi-tpi):
```
Olsem na yupela i mas beten olsem: 'Papa bilong mipela long heven...'
```
- Uses **mipela** (me+plural = we-EXCLUSIVE) {tpi-tpi}
- "Bilong mipela" = belonging to us (exclusive) {llm-cs45}

**Chamorro** (cha-cha):
```
Pot i taotao siha, para u manmañule' otro manera: 'Tatanmame gui i langet...'
```
- Uses **-mame** suffix (our-EXCLUSIVE) {cha-cha}
- Chamorro marks clusivity with pronouns

/suffixes {llm-cs45}

**Cebuano** (ceb-ULB):
```
Pag-ampo ninyo sa ingon niini, 'Amahan namo nga anaa sa langit...'
```
- Uses **namo** (our-EXCLUSIVE, from kami) {ceb-ULB}
- Would use *nato* (from kita) if God were included {llm-cs45}

## Patterns Identified

### 1. **Direct Speaker-Addressee Contrast**
When "we/us" is explicitly contrasted with "you", EXCLUSIVE is required:
- "**We** speak... **you** do not receive **our** testimony" (John 3:11)
- "**We**... send to **you**" (Acts 15:25)

### 2. **Prayer Context**
When praying TO God, "we/us/our" cannot include God (the addressee):
- "**Our** Father" [addressed to God] (Matthew 6:9)
- "Do not remember against **us**" [praying to God] (Psalm 79:8)

### 3. **Epistolary Structure**
Letter senders ("we") are distinct from letter recipients ("you"):
- Acts 15:25, 1 Corinthians 1:23, most NT epistles

### 4. **Group Boundary Marking**
When speaker represents one group addressing another group:
- "Let **us** [Israelites] go" [to Pharaoh] (Exodus 3:18)
- "**We** preach" [apostles to church] (1 Corinthians 1:23)

### 5. **Authority/Role Distinction**
When speaker has specialized role distinct from addressees:
- Apostles → churches
- Prophet → people
- Teacher → students

## Translation Guidance

### For Clusivity-Marking Languages

When you encounter these patterns, **USE EXCLUSIVE forms**:

| Language | EXCLUSIVE (use) | Inclusive (avoid) |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Tagalog** | kami, namin, amin | tayo, natin, atin |
| **Indonesian** | kami | kita |
| **Cebuano** | kami, namo, amo | kita, nato, atong |
| **Tok Pisin** | mipela | yumi |
| **Hawaiian** | makou | kakou |
| **Chamorro** | -mame/-maime | -ta |

### Common Errors to Avoid

❌ **Using inclusive in prayer to God**:
- Wrong: Matthew 6:9 with *kita* (would illogically include God in "our Father")
- Right: Matthew 6:9 with *kami* (excludes God, who is being addressed)

❌ **Using inclusive when explicitly contrasted with "you"**:
- Wrong: John 3:11 with *tayo* (contradicts "we... but you")
- Right: John 3:11 with *kami* (maintains speaker/addressee distinction)

❌ **Using inclusive in epistolary sender/recipient structure**:
- Wrong: Acts 15:25 with *yumi* (confuses who is writing vs receiving letter)
- Right: Acts 15:25 with *mipela* (maintains sender/recipient boundary)

## Special Case: Isaiah 6:8

**Note**: Isaiah 6:8 shows theological ambiguity with split interpretation:
- **Indonesian** (1 language): Uses kami (EXCLUSIVE) - God addressing prophet
- **Tagalog/Cebuano/Tok Pisin** (3+ languages): Use INCLUSIVE - Divine council addressing each other

This represents genuine theological disagreement about whether Isaiah is the direct addressee or an overhearer of divine council deliberation. See [ISA-006-008.md](ISA-006-008.md) for full analysis.

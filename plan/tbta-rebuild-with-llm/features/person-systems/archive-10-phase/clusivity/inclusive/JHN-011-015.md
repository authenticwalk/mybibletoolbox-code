# John 11:15 - Inclusive Clusivity Analysis

## Verse Overview

**Reference**: John 11:15
**English (eng-LSV)**: "and I rejoice, for your sake (that you may believe), that I was not there; but **we** may go to him"

**Context**: Jesus is speaking to his disciples after learning about Lazarus's death. He proposes that they (Jesus + disciples) go to Lazarus together.

**Genre**: Gospel (Narrative discourse)

**Participants**:
- **Speaker**: Jesus (1st person)
- **Addressees**: The disciples (2nd person)
- **Referent in "us/we"**: Jesus + disciples (1st person plural)

**Why INCLUSIVE**: The phrase "let us go to him" includes both the speaker (Jesus) and the addressees (the disciples) in the action of going. Jesus is proposing that he will go with them, making this a clear case of inclusive clusivity.

## Source Text Analysis

### Greek (grc-SR, grc-TCGNT)
```
Καὶ χαίρω δι᾽ ὑμᾶς, ἵνα πιστεύσητε, ὅτι οὐκ ἤμην ἐκεῖ· ἀλλὰ ἄγωμεν πρὸς αὐτόν.
```

**Key phrase**: **ἄγωμεν** (agōmen) = "let us go"
- **Form**: 1st person plural subjunctive of ἄγω (to lead, go)
- **Function**: Hortatory subjunctive expressing exhortation/proposal
- **Clusivity**: Greek does not grammatically mark inclusive vs. exclusive distinction
- **Semantic analysis**: Context makes clear that Jesus includes himself in the action (he will go with the disciples to Lazarus)

**Citation**: {grc-SR}, {grc-TCGNT}

## Recommendations for Translators

### For Languages with Grammatical Clusivity

**Recommendation**: **Use INCLUSIVE forms** for John 11:15 "let us go to him"

**Why**:
1. **Speaker-addressee inclusion**: Jesus explicitly includes the disciples in the proposed action
2. **Cross-linguistic consensus**: All 7 clusivity-marking languages examined use inclusive forms
3. **Contextual support**: Jesus is not saying "I will go" or "you should go," but "let us [you and I together] go"

**Specific guidance by language family**:

**Austronesian - Philippine**:
- Tagalog: Use **tayo** (not *kami*)
- Cebuano: Use **kita** (not *kami*)
- Ilocano: Use **-tayo** affixes (not *-kami*)

**Austronesian - Malayo-Polynesian**:
- Indonesian/Malay: Use **kita** (not *kami*)

**Austronesian - Oceanic**:
- Tongan: Use **tau** (not *mau*)
- Hawaiian: Use **kakou** (not *makou*)
- Fijian: Use **kedarau/kedatou** (not *keirau/keitou*)

### For Languages without Grammatical Clusivity

**Recommendation**: Ensure translation choices support inclusive interpretation

**Strategies**:
1. Use hortatory/jussive forms: "let us," "vayamos," "allons"
2. Avoid interpretive additions that might suggest exclusion
3. Maintain focus on Jesus's proposal to accompany the disciples

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

**Pitfall 1**: Using exclusive forms in clusivity languages
- **Error**: "We [excluding you, disciples] will go"
- **Result**: Contradicts the narrative context where Jesus goes with the disciples

**Pitfall 2**: Ambiguous translations in non-clusivity languages
- **Error**: "I will go" or "You should go"
- **Result**: Obscures the inclusive nature of Jesus's proposal

**Pitfall 3**: Inconsistent translation within same language
- **Error**: Using different clusivity values for parallel verses (e.g., John 11:7, 11:15, 11:16)
- **Result**: Creates confusing participant reference patterns

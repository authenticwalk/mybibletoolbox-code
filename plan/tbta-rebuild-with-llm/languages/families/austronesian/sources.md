# Sources and References

This document lists all sources cited in the Austronesian language family research, organized by category.

## Primary Scholarly Sources

### Classification and Subgrouping

**Blust, Robert (1977)**
- "The Proto-Austronesian Pronouns and Austronesian Subgrouping: A Preliminary Report."
- *Working Papers in Linguistics, University of Hawaii* 9(2): 1-15.
- **Supports**: Ten primary branches theory, Formosan diversity

**Blust, Robert (1999)**
- "Subgrouping, circularity and extinction: some issues in Austronesian comparative linguistics."
- In E. Zeitoun & P. J.-k. Li (eds.), *Selected Papers from the Eighth International Conference on Austronesian Linguistics*, 31-94.
- Taipei: Academia Sinica.
- **Supports**: Current classification model, Malayo-Polynesian grouping

**Blust, Robert (2009)**
- *The Austronesian Languages*
- Canberra: Pacific Linguistics.
- **Supports**: First single-authored book covering all aspects of Austronesian

### Voice and Alignment

**Schachter, Paul (1987)**
- *Tagalog*
- In Bernard Comrie (ed.), *The World's Major Languages*, 936-958.
- Oxford: Oxford University Press.
- **Supports**: Proposed "trigger" terminology for voice systems

**Aldridge, Edith (2016)**
- "Ergativity from Subjunctive in Austronesian Languages."
- *Language* 92(1).
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1606822X15613499
- **Supports**: Reanalysis of irrealis clause types leading to non-accusative alignment

### Austronesian Comparative Dictionary

**Blust, Robert & Steve Trussel**
- *Austronesian Comparative Dictionary* (ACD)
- Online: https://acd.clld.org/
- Also: https://www.trussel2.com/ACD/
- **Supports**: 119,768 words from 1,022 language varieties; 8,161 cognate sets

**Blust, Robert & Steve Trussel (2013)**
- "The Austronesian Comparative Dictionary: A Work in Progress."
- *Oceanic Linguistics* 52(2): 493-523.
- URL: https://muse.jhu.edu/article/530554
- **Supports**: Methodology and scope of ACD project

### Formosan Languages

**Zeitoun, Elizabeth & Paul Jen-kuei Li (eds.) (1999)**
- *Selected Papers from the Eighth International Conference on Austronesian Linguistics*
- Taipei: Academia Sinica.
- **Supports**: Formosan language features and diversity

**Ross, Malcolm (2009)**
- "Proto-Austronesian Verbal Morphology: A Reappraisal."
- In Alexander Adelaar & Andrew Pawley (eds.), *Austronesian Historical Linguistics and Culture History: A Festschrift for Robert Blust*, 295-326.
- Canberra: Pacific Linguistics.
- **Supports**: Verbal morphology reconstruction

### Philippine Languages

**Schachter, Paul & Fe T. Otanes (1972)**
- *Tagalog Reference Grammar*
- Berkeley: University of California Press.
- **Supports**: Tagalog grammar and voice system details

**Delmer, Jean-Francois (2018)**
- "Philippine-type Focus Systems of Five Manobo Languages."
- URL: https://www.academia.edu/38054811/
- **Supports**: Manobo language focus systems

### Oceanic Languages

**Lynch, John, Malcolm Ross & Terry Crowley (2002)**
- *The Oceanic Languages*
- Richmond: Curzon Press.
- **Supports**: Oceanic language features and classification

**Palmer, Bill (ed.) (2017)**
- *The Languages and Linguistics of the New Guinea Area: A Comprehensive Guide*
- Berlin: De Gruyter Mouton.
- **Supports**: PNG language features, contact effects

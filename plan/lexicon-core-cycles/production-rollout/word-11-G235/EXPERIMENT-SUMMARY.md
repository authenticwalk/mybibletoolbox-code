### Finding 2: οὐκ...ἀλλά Pattern is Paul's Soteriological Tool ✅

**Discovery:** Triple use in Galatians 2:16 structures justification doctrine

**Pattern:** "not by works of law...but by faith in Christ" (3× in one verse)

**Theological applications:**
- Grace vs. Works (Gal 2:16, Rom 3:28, Eph 2:8-9)
- Law vs. Grace (Rom 6:14, Gal 3:23-25)
- Flesh vs. Spirit (Rom 8:4-9, Gal 5:16-18)
- Human initiative vs. Divine action

**Implication:** ἀλλά is essential for understanding Paul's replacement theology

### Finding 3: Runge's "Corrective Constraint" Unifies ἀλλά's Functions ✅

**Runge's insight:** ἀλλά uniquely "corrects some aspect of what precedes"

**Unifying power:**
- Explains adversative uses (corrects false with true)
- Explains exclusionary uses (corrects inclusion with exception)
- Explains ascensive uses (corrects insufficient with sufficient)
- Explains theological uses (corrects human with divine)

**Pedagogical value:** Students can ask "What is being corrected?" to unlock ἀλλά's function

**Implication:** Modern discourse grammar complements traditional approaches excellently

### Finding 4: ἀλλά's Non-Postpositive Nature Increases Rhetorical Force ✅

**Key distinction from δέ:**
- δέ must be postpositive (never first in clause)
- ἀλλά can be clause-initial (often first for emphasis)

**Rhetorical implications:**
- Clause-initial position maximizes discourse impact
- Creates "sharp pivot from human assumption to God-given truth" (BibleHub)
- Allows strong replies, emphatic objections, major contrasts

**Implication:** Positional freedom contributes to ἀλλά's strength vs. δέ's weakness

### Finding 5: 74% Frequency Increase (LXX → NT) Reflects Theological Loading ✅

**Data:**
- LXX: 367 occurrences

- Increase: +74% relative to corpus size

**Interpretation:**

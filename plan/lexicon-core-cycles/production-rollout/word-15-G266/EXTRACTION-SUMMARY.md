### 7. Controversies (6 Major Debates)

**1. "Missing the Mark" Etymology**
- **Debate:** Helpful pedagogical tool vs. misleading archaic etymology
- **Proponents:** Emphasize falling short of God's standard
- **Critics:** Obsolete by NT period, misleading to claim as "literal meaning"
- **Consensus:** Historically accurate but not primary NT usage

**2. Pelagian Controversy (5th century)**
- **Pelagius:** Rejected original sin, free will to achieve perfection
- **Augustine:** Fall impaired human nature, original sin transmitted
- **Outcome:** Pelagianism condemned at Council of Carthage (418 CE)
- **Impact:** Shaped all subsequent soteriological debates

**3. Sin as Personification (Romans 5-7)**
- **Literary Device View:** Rhetorical technique, not ontological claim
- **Cosmic Power View:** Sin as apocalyptic force with real agency
- **Implications:** Affects anthropology, soteriology, sanctification theology

**4. Sin as State vs. Acts**
- **State Emphasis:** Sin as ontological condition (Augustinian original sin)
- **Acts Emphasis:** Sin as volitional choices (human responsibility)
- **Integrated View:** Both/and - not either/or

**5. Johannine Sinlessness (1 John)**
- **Habitual Sin View:** Christians sin occasionally but not habitually (most popular)
- **Sinless Perfection View:** Born of God = sinless nature
- **Two Natures View:** Old nature sins, new nature doesn't
- **Forensic View:** Sin not imputed positionally in Christ

**6. Sin Offering Translation**
- **Abstract:** "For sin" or "concerning sin" - general reference
- **Technical:** "Sin offering" - Levitical technical term (peri hamartias)
- **Contextual:** Translation depends on context (Hebrews 10 clearly technical)

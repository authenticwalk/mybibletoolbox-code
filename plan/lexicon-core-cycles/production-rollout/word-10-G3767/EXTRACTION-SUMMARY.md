### Functional Range
1. **Inferential/Illative** - Marks logical consequence (therefore, consequently)
2. **Resumptive/Epanaleptic** - Resumes discourse after interruption (so then, well then)
3. **Transitional/Continuative** - Connects sequential events (then, so)
4. **Exhortatory/Hortatory** - Bridges doctrine to application (therefore, so then)
5. **Interrogative** - Introduces rhetorical questions (what then? why then?)

- Functions primarily as narrative connective with weakened inferential force
- Scholarly explanation: "oral residue" from conversational discourse embedded in written text

- Bridges indicative (what God did) to imperative (what we should do)
- Key passages: Romans 5:1, 6:12, 8:1, 12:1; Ephesians 4:1

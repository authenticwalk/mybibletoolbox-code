## Word Overview

**G3767 οὖν** - Particle (inferential, postpositive)

- **Occurrences:** 499 (NA28), 525 (TR), 128 (LXX)
- **Primary Function:** Marks logical consequence/inference (therefore, then, so)
- **Ranking:** Among most common NT words
- **Key Significance:** Commonest inferential conjunction in NT

---

## Major Findings

### 1. **Exceptional Theological Significance**
οὖν plays crucial role in NT theology:
- **Pauline argumentation:** Structures doctrine → application transitions
- **Indicative-imperative bridge:** What God did → what we should do
- **Τί οὖν diatribe:** 15+ rhetorical questions in Romans alone
- **Key passages:** Romans 5:1, 6:12, 8:1, 12:1; Ephesians 4:1

### 2. **Genre-Specific Functional Variation**
- **John's Gospel (200+ times):** Weakened inferential force; narrative connector like δέ
  - Scholarly explanation: "oral residue" from conversational discourse
- **Pauline Epistles:** Strong inferential force; diatribe-style reasoning
- **Acts:** Resumptive function (μέν οὖν 17 times)

### 3. **Diachronic Development Well-Documented**
- **Homer:** 84 occurrences; undeveloped inferential force
- **Classical (Herodotus+):** Inferential function established
- **Koine/NT:** Commonest inferential particle
- **Scholarly sources:** Muchnová 2017 (Homeric), Frame-Poythress (Johannine)

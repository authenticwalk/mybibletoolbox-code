### 2. Controversies Documented (4 major debates)

**A. John 1:1 Translation Debate**
- **Claim:** "The Word was a god" vs "the Word was God" (anarthrous θεός issue)
- **Context:** Jehovah's Witnesses, Arian groups, some academics
- **Refutation:** Colwell's Rule (1933), syntactic analysis, contextual evidence
- **Consensus:** Overwhelming support for traditional "was God" translation

**B. Background Sources Debate**
- **Spectrum:** Hellenistic philosophy (Philo) vs Jewish Wisdom vs Hebrew dabar
- **Positions:** Strong Philonic dependence vs minimal external influence
- **Current view:** Multivalent background, John writing for diverse audience
- **Key distinction:** Johannine Logos becomes incarnate (1:14), Philo's cannot

**C. Logos/Rhema False Distinction**
- **Claim:** Logos = written Word, rhema = spoken word (Word of Faith teaching)
- **Refutation:** Biblical evidence (Matt 26:75 vs Luke 22:61 use terms interchangeably)
- **Trench's actual distinction:** λαλέω (articulation) vs λέγω (meaningful content)
- **Danger:** Subjective "rhema-revelations" trump biblical authority

**D. Pre-incarnate Logos Personhood**
- **Question:** When did concept of personal pre-existent Logos develop?
- **Claim:** Justin Martyr (c. 150 AD) introduced, not original to John
- **Counter:** John 1:1-3 requires personal pre-existence (agency, distinct personhood)
- **Context:** Early church development of Christological categories

**Assessment:** Systematic controversy search yielded 4 substantive debates with
comprehensive scholarly refutation. Exceeds typical 3-controversy target.

### 3. Diachronic Development (5 stages)

**Stage 1: Proto-Indo-European**
- Root: *leǵ- "to gather, collect, put in order, speak"

**Stage 2: Classical Greek (800-300 BCE)**

- Heraclitus (c. 535-475 BC): First philosophical use - cosmic ordering principle
- Plato (427-347 BC): Logos as soul of existence, inward thought vs outward expression
- Aristotle (384-322 BC): Rules of rational thought ("logic"), modes of persuasion
- LSJ documents 10 major headings, 60+ translations

**Stage 3: Hellenistic Period (300 BCE - 1st c. CE)**
- Stoics: Divine reason ordering universe, logos spermatikos (generative principle)
- Philo of Alexandria (20 BC - 50 AD): Syncretized Stoic logos + Jewish Scripture,
  quasi-personal divine intermediary, identified with Wisdom and Angel of Lord
- Documentary papyri: Commercial, legal, administrative usage

**Stage 4: Septuagint (3rd-1st c. BCE)**
- Translates Hebrew דָּבָר (dabar) 943 times

- Key texts: Psalm 33:6, Genesis 1, prophetic formulas
- Established λόγος as standard for God's creative/prophetic/covenantal word

**Stage 5: New Testament (1st c. CE)**
- **Synoptics:** Jesus' teaching, gospel proclamation, parables, reports
- **Johannine:** Revolutionary Christology - eternal, personal, divine, incarnate Logos
- **Pauline:** Gospel message ("word of cross/reconciliation/faith"), Christ as "wisdom"
- **Acts:** Apostolic proclamation, church growth through "the word"
- **General Epistles:** Life-giving, saving, living and active word

**Trajectory:** "Gathering" → "Cosmic rational principle" → "Divine intermediary"

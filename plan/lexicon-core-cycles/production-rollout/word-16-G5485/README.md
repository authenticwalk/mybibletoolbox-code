## Core Findings

### Etymology
- **Root:** χαίρω (G5463 - to rejoice)
- **Indo-European:** *gher- (pleasure from good social standing, 4000-year lineage)
- **LXX Usage:** Translates Hebrew חֵן (hen - favor) 61-67 times

### Primary Meaning
Divine grace/favor freely bestowed in salvation, opposing works-based justification (Rom.11:6, Eph.2:8-9).

### Secondary Meanings
1. Favor (divine or human)
2. Gratitude/thanksgiving
3. Gracious gifts/benefits
4. Charm of speech
5. Greco-Roman patronage

## Theological Significance

**Pauline Centrality:**
- 81+ uses in Pauline corpus (51% of NT total)
- Progressive development: Galatians (early) → Romans (middle) → Ephesians (later)
- Central to soteriology: grace vs. works, grace vs. law

**Key Texts:**
- Rom.11:6 - "if by grace, no longer by works"
- Eph.2:8-9 - "saved by grace through faith, not works"
- 2Cor.12:9 - "My grace is sufficient for you"

# Cycle 4: Quality Recovery with Minimal Time Cost

**Date:** 2025-11-11
**Status:** Planning
**Focus:** Recover grammatical pathway richness while maintaining Cycle 3 efficiency gains

---

## Executive Summary

**Problem:** Cycle 3 achieved excellent time savings (-33%) but richness fell short of target (8.25/10 vs 8.9-9.1 target, -8% from Cycle 2 overall average). Analysis shows richness loss is entirely concentrated in the grammatical pathway due to aggressive morphology reduction and diachronic streamlining.

**Solution:** Implement **2 targeted refinements** for grammatical pathway words to recover 50-100% of richness loss at minimal time cost (+4 min per word).

**Philosophy:** Find the sweet spot between Cycle 2's quality (9.0/10 overall) and Cycle 3's efficiency (-33% time). This is a **minor refinement cycle**, not major changes.

## 2 Core Refinements (Grammatical Pathway Only)

### Refinement 1: Enhanced Morphology Coverage

**Current State (Cycle 3):**
- Forms documented: Top 10
- Coverage: 85% of occurrences (4,757/5,597 for G846)
- Time: 8 minutes
- Richness contribution: 1.2/1.5 pts

**Problem:** Too aggressive - missed important forms that account for 15% of usage

**Proposed (Cycle 4):**
- Forms documented: **Top 15**
- Coverage: **90-92%** of occurrences (estimated 5,050/5,597)
- Time: **10 minutes (+2 min)**
- Richness contribution: **1.35/1.5 pts (+0.15 pts)**

**Rationale:**
- Pareto principle: 10 was too strict, 15 hits sweet spot
- Covers an additional 5-7 percentage points of usage
- Still efficient compared to Cycle 2's 49 forms (all forms)
- Applies to **ultra-high frequency grammatical words only** (1,000+ occurrences)

**Implementation Details:**
1. Update grammatical pathway template to request top 15 forms
2. Include exact occurrence counts for each form
3. Document total coverage percentage
4. Maintain parallel extraction optimization

**Expected Impact:**
- **Time cost:** +2 minutes per grammatical word
- **Richness gain:** +0.15 points (50% recovery of morphology loss)
- **Coverage improvement:** +5-7 percentage points
- **ROI:** 0.075 pts/min (EXCELLENT)

---

### Refinement 2: Enriched Diachronic Analysis

**Current State (Cycle 3):**
- Data points: 5 bullet points (frequency shifts only)
- Focus: When word became common/rare in different periods
- Time: 3 minutes
- Richness contribution: 0.9/1.0 pts

**Problem:** Too narrow - missed semantic stability patterns valuable for students

**Proposed (Cycle 4):**
- Data points: **7 bullet points**
- Focus: **Frequency-focused + semantic stability notes**
- Add: Genre distribution patterns, textual variant patterns
- Time: **5 minutes (+2 min)**
- Richness contribution: **1.0/1.0 pts (+0.1 pts)**

**Rationale:**
- Not full semantic development (that's too time-intensive)
- Add lightweight context: genre preferences, stability across manuscripts
- Still frequency-focused, just richer context
- Applies to **all grammatical words**

**Implementation Details:**
1. Update grammatical pathway template for 7 diachronic points
2. Include: Frequency trajectory (3 points) + Genre distribution (2 points) + Variant patterns (2 points)
3. Keep analysis concise (1-2 sentences per point)
4. Leverage existing source data (no new WebFetch calls)

**Expected Impact:**
- **Time cost:** +2 minutes per grammatical word
- **Richness gain:** +0.1 points (100% recovery of diachronic loss)
- **Pedagogical value:** Better historical context for translators
- **ROI:** 0.05 pts/min (GOOD)

## Key Principles for Cycle 4

### Design Philosophy

1. **Minor Refinement, Not Major Overhaul:**
   - Only 2 changes to grammatical pathway
   - Everything else stays from Cycle 3
   - Surgical precision, not broad changes

2. **Grammatical Focus Only:**
   - Theological pathway is perfect (zero loss in C3)
   - All refinements target grammatical words
   - Don't fix what isn't broken

3. **Sweet Spot Targeting:**
   - Balance between C2 quality and C3 efficiency
   - Not trying to fully restore C2 richness
   - Not maintaining C3's aggressive cuts
   - Target: 85% of C2 quality at 70% of C2 time

4. **Empirical Validation:**
   - Test on 3 diverse words
   - Measure time and richness precisely
   - Compare to C3 baselines directly
   - Let data drive decisions

### Quality Standards

1. **Non-Negotiable:**
   - 100% validation (all critical items)
   - Zero fabrication
   - Inline citations for all claims
   - Source quality maintained

2. **Optimization Focus:**
   - Morphology coverage: 90-92% (was 85%)
   - Diachronic depth: 7 points (was 5)
   - Time: 53-55 min average (was 50.5)
   - Richness: 8.4-8.5/10 (was 8.25)

3. **Transparency:**
   - Document coverage percentages
   - Explain what's included/excluded
   - Clear rationale for all limits
   - Honest about trade-offs

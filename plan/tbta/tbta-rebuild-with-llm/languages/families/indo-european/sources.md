## 14. Note on Source Quality

**Primary sources:** Academic publications, university press books, journal articles
**Secondary sources:** Encyclopedia articles (Britannica, Encyclopedia.com)
**Tertiary sources:** Wikipedia (used for general overviews, verified against primary sources)
**Educational sources:** MasterRussian.com, Study-Languages-Online.com (for examples)

**Reliability assessment:**
- Academic sources (peer-reviewed): High reliability
- Reference works (Britannica, Cambridge UP): High reliability
- Wikipedia: Moderate reliability (useful for overviews, should verify claims)
- Educational websites: Moderate reliability (good for examples, less for theory)

**Recommendations for verification:**
- Claims marked [NEEDS SOURCE] should be verified against primary sources
- Wikipedia citations should be traced to original sources when possible
- Numerical claims (speaker numbers, dates, counts) should be verified

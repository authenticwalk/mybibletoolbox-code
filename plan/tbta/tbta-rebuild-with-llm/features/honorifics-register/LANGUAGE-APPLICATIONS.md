# Language-Specific Applications of Speaker Demographics

How to apply the 6 speaker demographics features in major languages with honorific systems. This guide provides concrete translation mappings for Japanese, Korean, Javanese, Thai, Vietnamese, Hindi/Bengali, and others.

---

## Japanese (jpn) - Highly Complex System

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Highest)
**Obligatory**: Yes (grammatically required in most contexts)
**Number of Levels**: 4+ major levels (casual, polite, honorific, humble)
**Primary Drivers**: Age, social status, context, gender

### Keigo System (敬語)

| Register | Name | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|----------|------|-------|--------------|
| Kudaketa (崩した) | Casual/Broken | Close friends, family | Attitude=F, Age Rel=S, Style=Informal |
| Teineigo (丁寧語) | Polite | Standard polite (-masu/-desu) | Attitude=n/P, Style=Formal |
| Sonkeigo (尊敬語) | Respectful/Honorific | Superiors, elders, customers | Attitude=H, Age Rel=Y |
| Kenjougo (謙譲語) | Humble/Modest | About oneself to superiors | Attitude=H (speaker lowers self) |

### Feature Mapping

#### Speaker's Attitude → Keigo Selection

| TBTA Attitude | Japanese Register | Verb Forms | Example |
|---------------|-------------------|------------|---------|
| F (Familiar) | Kudaketa | Plain forms (食べる taberu) | Jesus to close disciples in private |
| n (Neutral) | Teineigo | -masu/-desu (食べます tabemasu) | Jesus teaching crowd |
| P (Polite) | Teineigo+ | -masu/-desu + respectful vocab | Centurion to Jesus |
| H (Honorable) | Sonkeigo/Kenjougo | Honorific verbs (召し上がる meshiagaru) | Prayer to God, elder to youth |

#### Age Relationship → Register Shifts

| Age Relationship | Japanese Adjustment | Example |
|------------------|---------------------|---------|
| O (Speaker Older significant) | Can use kudaketa to listener | Parent to child, teacher to student |
| o (Speaker Older slight) | Typically teineigo, can be casual | Older friend to younger |
| S (Same Age) | Depends on familiarity (kudaketa or teineigo) | Peers, same-age friends |
| y (Speaker Younger slight) | Must use teineigo minimum | Younger to older colleague |
| Y (Speaker Younger significant) | Must use sonkeigo/kenjougo | Youth to elder, student to teacher |

#### Speech Style → Context Modifiers

| Speech Style | Japanese Context | Additional Markers |
|--------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Liturgical | Prayer keigo | Special humble forms for addressing God |
| Prophetic | Literary elevated | Classical grammar, elevated vocabulary |
| Didactic | Teaching register | Explanatory forms, -masu/-desu |
| Informal | Casual conversation | Plain forms, casual particles |
| Formal | Public speech | Complete sentences, teineigo throughout |

### Gender and Particles

**Male Speakers**:
- Assertive particles: ぞ (zo), だ (da), な (na)
- Self-reference: 僕 (boku) casual, 俺 (ore) very casual, 私 (watashi) formal

**Female Speakers**:
- Soft particles: わ (wa), ね (ne), よ (yo)
- Self-reference: 私 (watashi), あたし (atashi) casual
- Traditional: More polite forms expected

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: God to Moses (Exodus 3:5)
- Features: Speaker=M (God), Listener=S (Moses), Attitude=H, Age=N, Style=Formal
- **Japanese**:
  - Use highest sonkeigo for Moses (お取りなさい o-tori nasai "please remove")
  - Use humble/reverent vocabulary for sacred space
  - Formal complete sentences with -masu/-desu
  - Address Moses respectfully despite being subordinate

#### Example 2: Jesus to Disciples (John 13:33)
- Features: Speaker=R (Jesus), Listener=D (Disciples), Attitude=F, Age=S, Style=Informal
- **Japanese**:
  - Can use kudaketa (casual) forms at Last Supper
  - Address disciples with familiar 君 (kimi "you") or names
  - Plain verb forms acceptable (行く iku instead of 行きます ikimasu)
  - Warm particles (ね ne, よ yo)

#### Example 3: Peter's Sermon (Acts 2:14)
- Features: Speaker=P (Peter), Listener=C (Crowd), Attitude=n, Age=S, Style=Formal
- **Japanese**:
  - Public speech requires teineigo throughout
  - Complete -masu/-desu sentences
  - Respectful vocabulary for audience (皆様 minasama)
  - Formal question forms (-ますか masuka)

## Korean (kor) - Grammatically Obligatory System

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Highest)
**Obligatory**: Yes (every verb must be marked)
**Number of Levels**: 6-7 speech levels
**Primary Drivers**: Age, social status, formality context

### Speech Levels (Honoring System)

| Level | Name | Verb Ending | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|-------|------|-------------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Hasyoche (하십시오체) | -습니다/-ㅂ니다 | Highest formal | Attitude=H, Style=Formal |
| 2 | Hasosoche (하오체) | -오 | High formal (archaic) | Literary, prophetic |
| 3 | Haoche (하게체) | -게 | Addressee lowering | Not common in Bible |
| 4 | Haeoche (해요체) | -어요/-아요 | Polite informal | Attitude=n/P |
| 5 | Haerache (해라체) | -다/-어라 | Familiar | Attitude=F (close) |
| 6 | Hageche (하게체) | -네 | Literary/nostalgic | Older texts |

### Simplified Modern System (Most Common)

| Register | Ending | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|----------|--------|-------|--------------|
| Jondaemal (존댓말) | -습니다/-ㅂ니다, -어요 | Respectful/formal | Attitude=H/P/n, Age Rel=Y |
| Banmal (반말) | -어/-아, plain forms | Casual/intimate | Attitude=F, Age Rel=O or S |

### Feature Mapping

#### Speaker's Attitude + Age Relationship → Speech Level

| Attitude | Age Rel | Korean Register | Verb Ending | Example Context |
|----------|---------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| H (Honorable) | Y (Younger to Elder) | Highest Jondaemal | -습니다/-ㅂ니다 | Prayer, respect to God/elders |
| P (Polite) | y (Younger slight) | Polite Jondaemal | -어요/-아요 | Polite conversation |
| n (Neutral) | S (Same Age) | Polite or Banmal | -어요 or -어 | Depends on familiarity |
| F (Familiar) | O (Older to Younger) | Banmal | -어/-아 | Parent to child, close friends |
| F (Familiar) | S (Peers) | Banmal | -어/-아 | Close same-age friends |

#### Speech Style → Register Choice

| Speech Style | Korean Application | Notes |
|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| Liturgical | Highest jondaemal + special prayer endings (-소서) | Addressing God |
| Formal | -습니다/-ㅂ니다 throughout | Public speech, official |
| Didactic | -습니다 or -어요 depending on audience | Teaching style |
| Informal | Banmal or -어요 | Private conversation |
| Prophetic | Literary forms, archaic jondaemal | Classical elevated style |

### Honorific Vocabulary

**Subject Honorification**:
- 먹다 (meokda) "eat" → 드시다 (deusida) "eat (honorific)"
- 자다 (jada) "sleep" → 주무시다 (jumusida) "sleep (honorific)"
- 있다 (itda) "be/have" → 계시다 (gyesida) "be/have (honorific)"

**Object Honorification**:
- 주다 (juda) "give" → 드리다 (deurida) "give (humble)"

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: Ruth to Naomi (Ruth 1:16)
- Features: Speaker=R (Ruth), Listener=n (Naomi), Attitude=E, Age=Y (Younger significant), Style=Informal
- **Korean**:
  - Must use jondaemal (존댓말) to mother-in-law
  - Respectful verb forms (가시다 gasida "go (hon.)" for Naomi)
  - Humble forms for self (가겠습니다 gagetseumnida "I will go")
  - -습니다 endings for respect despite intimate relationship
  - Warm vocabulary but formal grammar

#### Example 2: Jesus Teaching Disciples (Matthew 5:3)
- Features: Speaker=R (Jesus), Listener=D (Disciples/Crowd), Attitude=H, Age=S, Style=Didactic
- **Korean**:
  - Teaching register with -습니다/-ㅂ니다 endings
  - Can be slightly less formal than prayer (-어요 possible)
  - Respectful vocabulary for audience
  - Authoritative but accessible tone

#### Example 3: Peter and John (Friends, Acts)
- Features: Speaker=P (Peter), Listener=J (John), Attitude=F, Age=S, Style=Informal
- **Korean**:
  - Can use banmal (반말) between close same-age friends
  - Plain verb forms (-어/-아)
  - Casual particles
  - Still respectful given apostolic context

### Age Sensitivity

**Critical in Korean**: Even 1 year age difference traditionally requires jondaemal from younger to older
- TBTA "S" (Same Age, ±4 years) may still require jondaemal in Korean culture
- Adjust based on Korean age reckoning (year of birth determines "same age")

## Javanese (jav) - Most Complex System

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Most complex among TBTA languages)
**Obligatory**: Yes (vocabulary and grammar differ by level)
**Number of Levels**: 10+ distinct levels
**Primary Drivers**: Age, status, familiarity, context, descent

### Major Speech Levels

| Level | Name | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|-------|------|-------|--------------|
| Ngoko Lugu | Plain ngoko | Intimate equals, adults to children | Attitude=F, Age Rel=O |
| Ngoko Andhap | Humble ngoko | Adding humble forms | Attitude=n, Age Rel=S |
| Madya Ngoko | Mid-casual | Slight respect | Attitude=n, Age Rel=o |
| Madya Krama | Mid-formal | Standard respect | Attitude=P, Age Rel=y |
| Krama | Polite | General respect | Attitude=H, Age Rel=Y (slight) |
| Krama Inggil | High polite | High respect, elders | Attitude=H, Age Rel=Y (significant) |
| Krama Desa | Village polite | Rural polite forms | Contextual |

### Simplified 3-Level System (Most Common Teaching)

| Level | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| Ngoko | Familiar/Low | Attitude=F, Age Rel=O or S (close) |
| Madya | Middle | Attitude=n/P, Age Rel=y |
| Krama (incl. Krama Inggil) | Polite/High | Attitude=H, Age Rel=Y |

### Feature Mapping

#### Combined Feature Decision Matrix

| Attitude | Age Rel | Status | Javanese Level | Example Context |
|----------|---------|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| H | Y | Divine/Elder | Krama Inggil | Prayer, addressing God |
| H | Y | Authority | Krama | Youth to teacher, subject to king |
| P | y | Respectful | Madya Krama | Polite conversation with slight elder |
| n | S | Peer | Madya or Ngoko | Colleagues, acquaintances |
| F | S | Close friend | Ngoko | Intimate friends same age |
| F | O | Parent/Elder to Youth | Ngoko Lugu | Adult addressing child |

### Vocabulary Differences by Level

**Example: "to eat"**

| Level | Word | Usage |
|-------|------|-------|
| Ngoko | mangan | Casual, intimate |
| Madya | neda | Middle politeness |
| Krama | dhahar | Polite |
| Krama Inggil | nedha/dahar | High polite (for high-status person eating) |

**Many words change completely**:
- "I" (speaker): aku (ngoko) → kula (krama)
- "you" (listener): kowe (ngoko) → sampeyan (krama)
- "want": arep (ngoko) → ajeng (krama)

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: God to Moses (Exodus 3:5)
- Features: Speaker=M (God), Listener=S (Moses), Attitude=H, Age=N, Style=Formal
- **Javanese**:
  - Use Krama Inggil throughout
  - God's speech about Moses uses respectful krama vocabulary
  - Moses addressed with highest respect forms
  - Sacred context elevates register

#### Example 2: Disciples Among Themselves (Mark 9:34)
- Features: Speaker=D (Disciples), Listener=D (Disciples), Attitude=n/F, Age=S, Style=Informal
- **Javanese**:
  - Can use Ngoko (familiar) between peer friends
  - If not all equally close, Madya safer
  - Context (arguing) suggests familiarity allows Ngoko

#### Example 3: Youth to Elder Request
- Features: Attitude=H, Age=Y (significant), Style=Formal
- **Javanese**:
  - Must use Krama or Krama Inggil
  - Humble forms for speaker's actions
  - Respectful forms for elder's actions
  - Careful vocabulary selection throughout

## Thai (tha) - Particle and Pronoun System

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Obligatory**: Particles optional but culturally expected
**Number of Levels**: 3 major (high formal, standard polite, casual)
**Primary Drivers**: Age, status, gender, context (royal/monastic/secular)

### Pronoun System (Highly Complex)

**First Person (I)**:

| Pronoun | Formality | Gender | Usage | TBTA Context |
|---------|-----------|--------|-------|--------------|
| ผม (phom) | Formal | Male | Official, polite | Attitude=P/H, Male speaker |
| ดิฉัน (dichan) | Formal | Female | Official, polite | Attitude=P/H, Female speaker |
| ฉัน (chan) | Informal | Both | Casual, friends | Attitude=F/n |
| ชั้น (chan) | Casual | Female | Very casual | Attitude=F, informal |

**Second Person (You)**:

| Pronoun | Formality | Usage | TBTA Context |
|---------|-----------|-------|--------------|
| คุณ (khun) | Polite | Standard respectful | Attitude=n/P |
| ท่าน (than) | High formal | Honored person | Attitude=H, Elder |
| เธอ (thoe) | Informal | Friends, equals | Attitude=F, Age=S |
| (Name) | Common | Using name | Various contexts |

### Particle System

**Male Particles**:
- ครับ (khrap/krup) - Polite/formal
- สิ (si) - Casual emphasis
- (none) - Very casual

**Female Particles**:
- คะ (kha) - Polite question
- ค่ะ (kha) - Polite statement
- จ้ะ (ja) - Casual friendly
- (none) - Very casual

### Feature Mapping

#### Attitude + Gender → Particles

| Attitude | Gender | Particle | Example |
|----------|--------|----------|---------|
| H (Honorable) | Male | ครับ (khrap) | Every sentence to elder/authority |
| H (Honorable) | Female | คะ/ค่ะ (kha) | Every sentence to elder/authority |
| P (Polite) | Male | ครับ (khrap) | Standard polite |
| P (Polite) | Female | คะ/ค่ะ (kha) | Standard polite |
| n (Neutral) | Either | Optional particles | Context dependent |
| F (Familiar) | Either | Casual particles or none | Close friends |

#### Age Relationship → Pronoun Selection

| Age Rel | Speaker Pronoun | Listener Pronoun | Example |
|---------|----------------|------------------|---------|
| Y (Younger to Elder) | ผม/ดิฉัน (formal) | ท่าน/คุณ | Youth to elder |
| y (Younger slight) | ผม/ดิฉัน or ฉัน | คุณ | Polite peer |
| S (Same Age) | ฉัน (casual) | เธอ or name | Friends |
| O (Older to Younger) | Any appropriate | Casual or name | Adult to child |

#### Speech Style → Register

| Style | Thai Application | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Liturgical | Sacred register + formal particles | Addressing Buddha/divine |
| Formal | Formal pronouns + particles throughout | Public speech |
| Informal | Casual pronouns, optional particles | Private conversation |
| Royal | Ratchasap (ราชาศัพท์) - Special royal vocabulary | Kings, royal contexts |
| Monastic | Buddhist register | Monks, temple contexts |

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: Prayer to God (Matthew 6:9)
- Features: Speaker=T (Any), Listener=M (God), Attitude=H, Style=Liturgical
- **Thai**:
  - Use highest formal pronouns
  - Male: ผม (phom) + ครับ (khrap)
  - Female: ดิฉัน (dichan) + คะ/ค่ะ (kha)
  - Respectful vocabulary for God (พระเจ้า phra jao)
  - Complete formal sentences

#### Example 2: Jesus to Disciples Casual (John 13:33)
- Features: Speaker=R (Jesus), Listener=D (Disciples), Attitude=F, Style=Informal
- **Thai**:
  - Can use less formal pronouns (ฉัน chan possible)
  - May omit particles in intimate moments
  - Friendly vocabulary choices
  - Still respectful but warm tone

## Vietnamese (vie) - Kinship Pronoun System

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Obligatory**: Yes (pronoun choice is mandatory)
**Number of Levels**: Encoded in pronoun system
**Primary Drivers**: Age, relationship, familiarity

### Pronoun System (Kinship-Based)

**Age-Based Pronouns**:

| Pronoun | Literal Meaning | Usage | TBTA Mapping |
|---------|----------------|-------|--------------|
| ông | Grandfather | Elderly male (60+) | Speaker/Listener Age=D |
| bà | Grandmother | Elderly female (60+) | Age=D |
| anh | Older brother | Older male peer | Age Rel=y/o (speaker younger) |
| chị | Older sister | Older female peer | Age Rel=y/o (speaker younger) |
| em | Younger sibling | Younger person | Age Rel=O (speaker older) |
| con | Child | Child, or self in prayer | Speaker Age=A, or humble self to God |

**Formal Pronouns**:

| Pronoun | Usage | TBTA Context |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| tôi | I (neutral) | Formal speaker |
| bạn | You (neutral) | Formal listener peer |
| thầy | Teacher | Respectful for teacher/master |
| cô | Aunt/Ms. | Female authority figure |
| chúng ta | We (inclusive) | Group including listener |

### Feature Mapping

#### Age Relationship → Pronoun Selection (Critical)

| Age Rel | Speaker Uses (I) | Listener Addressed (You) | Example |
|---------|------------------|-------------------------|---------|
| Y (Younger significant) | con (child-like humility) or em | ông/bà (grandparent) | Youth to elder |
| y (Younger slight) | em | anh/chị | Younger to older peer |
| S (Same Age) | anh/chị (if slightly older) | em (if slightly younger) | Peers with slight difference |
| o (Older slight) | anh/chị | em | Older to younger peer |
| O (Older significant) | anh/chị or name | con/em | Adult to child |

#### Attitude → Additional Marking

| Attitude | Vietnamese Addition | Example |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| H (Honorable) | Add respectful titles (kính, quý) | Thưa ông (respectfully address) |
| P (Polite) | Formal complete sentences | Standard polite |
| n (Neutral) | Standard pronouns | Normal conversation |
| F (Familiar) | May use names, casual particles | Close friends |

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: Paul to Timothy (2 Timothy 1:2)
- Features: Speaker=P (Paul), Listener=t (Timothy), Attitude=E, Age=O (Older significant), Style=Informal
- **Vietnamese**:
  - Paul (older): anh or thầy (teacher)
  - Timothy (younger): em or con (student/spiritual child)
  - Warm vocabulary despite age difference
  - Can be less formal due to close relationship

#### Example 2: Ruth to Naomi (Ruth 1:16)
- Features: Speaker=R (Ruth), Listener=n (Naomi), Attitude=E, Age=Y (Younger), Style=Informal
- **Vietnamese**:
  - Ruth (daughter-in-law): con (child, showing respect)
  - Naomi (mother-in-law): mẹ (mother) or bà (grandmother-level respect)
  - Respectful yet warm pronouns
  - Complete sentences showing deference

#### Example 3: Disciples Peer Conversation
- Features: Attitude=F, Age=S, Style=Informal
- **Vietnamese**:
  - Slight age differences still require anh/em distinction
  - Can be casual but pronouns still encode who's slightly older
  - Friendly particles possible (nhé, ạ for politeness)

## Hindi (hin) and Bengali (ben) - Indo-Aryan Systems

### System Overview

**Complexity**: ⭐⭐⭐
**Obligatory**: Yes (pronoun and verb conjugation)
**Number of Levels**: 3 main (formal, neutral, intimate)
**Primary Drivers**: Age, status, familiarity

### Pronoun System (Hindi)

| Pronoun | Usage | Formality | TBTA Mapping |
|---------|-------|-----------|--------------|
| आप (āp) | You (formal/respectful) | High | Attitude=H/P, Age Rel=Y |
| तुम (tum) | You (familiar) | Medium | Attitude=n/F, Age Rel=S |
| तू (tū) | You (intimate) | Low | Attitude=F, Age Rel=O (elder to youth) |
| मैं (main) | I | - | Speaker (neutral) |
| हम (ham) | We | - | Plural speaker |

### Verb Conjugation by Pronoun

**Example: "to go" (जाना jana)**

| Pronoun | Verb Form | Translation | Context |
|---------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| आप (āp) | जाइए (jāiye) | You go (respectful) | To elder/superior |
| तुम (tum) | जाओ (jāo) | You go (familiar) | To peer/friend |
| तू (tū) | जा (jā) | You go (intimate) | To child/very close |

### Feature Mapping

#### Attitude + Age Rel → Pronoun Selection

| Attitude | Age Rel | Hindi Pronoun | Verb Form | Example |
|----------|---------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| H | Y | आप (āp) | Formal conjugation | Prayer, youth to elder |
| P | y | आप (āp) | Formal | Polite conversation |
| n | S | तुम (tum) | Familiar | Peer friends |
| F | S | तू (tū) possible | Intimate | Very close friends |
| F | O | तुम (tum) or तू (tū) | Familiar | Parent to child |

### Translation Examples

#### Example 1: Prayer to God (Matthew 6:9)
- **Hindi**:
  - Use आप (āp) for God
  - Formal verb conjugations throughout
  - Respectful vocabulary (परमेश्वर parmeshwar for God)
  - -जी (-jī) suffix for extra respect

#### Example 2: Jesus Teaching (Matthew 5:3)
- **Hindi**:
  - Address crowd with आप (āp)
  - Formal teaching register
  - Clear instructional vocabulary
  - Respectful but accessible

#### Example 3: Close Disciples Conversing
- **Hindi**:
  - Can use तुम (tum) between friends
  - Familiar conjugations
  - Casual vocabulary
  - Still respectful tone

### Bengali Parallel

Bengali has similar system:
- আপনি (apni) = formal you (like Hindi आप)
- তুমি (tumi) = familiar you (like Hindi तुम)
- তুই (tui) = intimate you (like Hindi तू)

Mapping follows same principles as Hindi.

## Summary: Cross-Language Patterns

### Universal Patterns

1. **Divine Address**: All languages use highest respect register for prayer
2. **Age Hierarchy**: Younger to elder requires respect markers
3. **Familiarity Overrides**: Close relationships can lower formality (but not with elders)
4. **Gender Markers**: Many Asian languages have gender-specific particles/pronouns

### Feature Priority by Language

| Language | Most Critical Feature | Second Most Critical | Third |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Japanese | Speaker's Attitude | Age Relationship | Speech Style |
| Korean | Age Relationship | Speaker's Attitude | Speech Style |
| Javanese | Speaker's Attitude | Age Relationship | Social Status |
| Thai | Age Relationship | Gender (particles) | Speaker's Attitude |
| Vietnamese | Age Relationship | Speaker/Listener Identity | Attitude |
| Hindi/Bengali | Speaker's Attitude | Age Relationship | Speech Style |

### Common Translation Workflow

1. **Identify Speaker + Listener** (use SPEAKER-LISTENER-CODES.md)
2. **Determine Ages** (use AGE-RELATIONSHIP-GUIDE.md)
3. **Calculate Age Relationship**
4. **Assess Context** (genre, style, attitude)
5. **Select Language-Specific Register** (use tables above)
6. **Apply Grammar** (pronouns, verb endings, particles)
7. **Validate** (check consistency, cultural appropriateness)

---

**Note**: These are general guidelines. Actual translation requires native speaker consultation and cultural sensitivity. Regional variations within languages may differ from these standard patterns.

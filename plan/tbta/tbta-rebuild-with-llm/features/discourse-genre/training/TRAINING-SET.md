# Discourse Genre: Training Set

**Feature**: Discourse Genre
**Version**: 1.0
**Date**: 2025-11-11
**Total Verses**: 18 (2 per genre value)

---

## Training Set Design

**Coverage**: All 9 TBTA genre values with equal representation
- Climactic Narrative Story: 2 examples
- Background Narrative: 2 examples
- Procedural: 2 examples
- Expository: 2 examples
- Poetic: 2 examples
- Hortatory: 2 examples
- Prophetic: 2 examples
- Legal: 2 examples
- Epistolary: 2 examples

**Selection Criteria**:
- ✅ Equal coverage of all genre values (2 per value)
- ✅ Mix of OT (Hebrew, 50%) and NT (Greek, 50%)
- ✅ Mix of clear and moderately ambiguous cases
- ✅ Diverse books (no over-sampling from single book)
- ✅ Representative of typical genre contexts

## Training Verses

### 1. Climactic Narrative Story (Main storyline action)

#### GEN 1:3
**Text**: "And God said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Creation narrative - main action sequence
**Why Selected**: Clear narrative progression, simple structure

#### MAT 14:25
**Text**: "Shortly before dawn Jesus went out to them, walking on the lake."
**Language**: Greek
**Context**: Jesus walking on water - main narrative event
**Why Selected**: Gospel narrative, dramatic action

### 2. Background Narrative (Scene-setting, context)

#### GEN 1:2
**Text**: "Now the earth was formless and empty, darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Pre-creation state - scene-setting
**Why Selected**: Classic background description, establishes context

#### LUK 2:1
**Text**: "In those days Caesar Augustus issued a decree that a census should be taken of the entire Roman world."
**Language**: Greek
**Context**: Historical background for nativity
**Why Selected**: Setting the stage for main narrative

---

### 3. Procedural (Instructions, how-to sequences)

#### LEV 1:3
**Text**: "If the offering is a burnt offering from the herd, you are to offer a male without defect. You must present it at the entrance to the tent of meeting so that it will be acceptable to the LORD."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Sacrificial procedure - step-by-step instructions
**Why Selected**: Clear procedural structure with conditional + imperative

#### EXO 12:3
**Text**: "Tell the whole community of Israel that on the tenth day of this month each man is to take a lamb for his family, one for each household."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Passover preparation instructions
**Why Selected**: Ritual procedure with specific steps

### 4. Expository (Teaching, explanation, doctrine)

#### ROM 3:23

**Language**: Greek
**Context**: Pauline theological teaching
**Why Selected**: Clear doctrinal exposition, timeless present

#### MAT 5:14
**Text**: "You are the light of the world. A town built on a hill cannot be hidden."
**Language**: Greek
**Context**: Sermon on the Mount teaching
**Why Selected**: Jesus's teaching with metaphorical explanation

### 5. Poetic (Poetry, hymns, elevated language)

#### PSA 23:1
**Text**: "The LORD is my shepherd, I lack nothing."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Psalm of David
**Why Selected**: Classic Hebrew poetry, metaphorical language

#### LUK 1:46-47
**Text**: "My soul glorifies the Lord and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior."

### 6. Hortatory (Exhortation, appeal, persuasion)

#### HEB 12:1
**Text**: "Therefore, since we are surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses, let us throw off everything that hinders and the sin that so easily entangles. And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us."

**Context**: Exhortation to persevere
**Why Selected**: Classic hortatory discourse with "therefore...let us"

#### PHP 2:5
**Text**: "In your relationships with one another, have the same mindset as Christ Jesus."
**Language**: Greek
**Context**: Appeal for unity and humility
**Why Selected**: Direct exhortation with imperative force

### 7. Prophetic (Prophecy, divine utterance)

#### ISA 7:14
**Text**: "Therefore the Lord himself will give you a sign: The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call him Immanuel."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Messianic prophecy to Ahaz

#### JER 1:5
**Text**: "Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, before you were born I set you apart; I appointed you as a prophet to the nations."
**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Jeremiah's prophetic call
**Why Selected**: Divine speech, prophetic authority, elevated register

### 8. Legal (Laws, regulations, ordinances)

#### EXO 20:13

**Language**: Hebrew
**Context**: Ten Commandments

#### DEU 5:16
**Text**: "Honor your father and your mother, as the LORD your God has commanded you, so that you may live long and that it may go well with you in the land the LORD your God is giving you."

**Why Selected**: Legal command with consequence structure

### 9. Epistolary (Letter format, correspondence)

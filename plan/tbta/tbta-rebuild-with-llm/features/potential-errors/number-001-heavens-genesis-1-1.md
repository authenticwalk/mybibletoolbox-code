### 2. Source Text Analysis

#### Morphology
**Hebrew Form**: הַשָּׁמַיִם (ha-shamayim)
- Root: שָׁמַיִם (shamayim)
- Article: הַ (ha-, "the")
- Morphology: **-ayim suffix = dual ending**
- Pattern: Identical to dual nouns like עֵינַיִם (enayim, "eyes"), יָדַיִם (yadayim, "hands")

#### Semantics
**Lexical Analysis**:
1. BDB (Brown-Driver-Briggs): שָׁמַיִם always plural/dual form, meaning "heaven, sky, visible heavens"
2. HALOT: שָׁמַיִם (m. pl./du.) - "heaven(s), sky"
3. Semantic range:
   - Physical sky (visible heavens)
   - Abode of God (spiritual heavens)
   - Dual interpretation: "upper and lower heavens" OR generic plural

**Key Question**: Is this:
- **Dual**: Exactly two heavens (upper/lower, or ancient cosmology's firmament + waters above)
- **Plural**: Generic "heavens" (many, indefinite)
- **Singular**: Semantic concept of "the sky" as one entity

#### Grammatical Commentaries
1. **Gesenius' Hebrew Grammar §88**: Hebrew dual in -ayim often fossilized/lexicalized
2. **Waltke & O'Connor**: Dual in Hebrew restricted to pairs (body parts, time, natural pairs)
3. **Joüon-Muraoka**: שָׁמַיִם always takes plural verb forms but may be semantically singular

#### Interlinear Translations
- **ESV**: "heavens" (plural translation)
- **NIV**: "heavens" (plural translation)
- **NASB**: "heavens" (plural translation)
- **YLT**: "heavens" (plural translation)
- **Septuagint (LXX)**: τὸν οὐρανὸν (singular accusative!) - Greek translates as SINGULAR
- **Vulgate**: caelum (singular accusative!) - Latin translates as SINGULAR

**CRITICAL FINDING**: Both LXX and Vulgate translate Hebrew dual שָׁמַיִם as **SINGULAR** in Genesis 1:1!

### 3. Context Analysis

#### Preceding context
- Genesis 1:1 is opening verse (no prior context)

#### Following context
- Genesis 1:6-8: God creates רָקִיעַ (raqia, "expanse/firmament") to separate waters
- Genesis 1:8: "And God called the expanse **Heaven** [שָׁמָיִם]"
- Here, רָקִיעַ (singular) is **named** שָׁמָיִם (dual form)

#### Discourse structure
**Ancient Near Eastern Cosmology**:
- Two-tier system: Waters above firmament, waters below
- "Heavens" could refer to:
  1. The visible sky (one entity)
  2. Upper and lower regions (dual)
  3. Multiple heavens (Jewish tradition: 7 heavens)

**Theological factors**:
- Paul in 2 Cor 12:2 mentions "third heaven" (ἕως τρίτου οὐρανοῦ)
- Jewish tradition (Talmud): Seven heavens (שִׁבְעָה רְקִיעִים)
- Ancient understanding: Multiple levels vs. modern "sky" as one

#### Parallel passages
- **Psalm 8:3**: "When I consider **your heavens** [שָׁמֶיךָ]" - plural/dual form with singular "your"
- **Isaiah 51:6**: "Lift up your eyes to **the heavens** [הַשָּׁמַיִם]" - same form as Gen 1:1

# Rule 2.1 Challenge Verses: 25-Verse Selection

**Purpose**: Test corporate prayer/lament contexts that challenge Rule 2.1 (prayer excludes the one prayed to)
**Selection Date**: 2025-11-11
**Total Verses**: 25
**Excluded**: All existing test verse sets (47 verses from training, random, and adversarial)

---

## PSALMS (10 verses)

### 1. Psalm 14:1-3
**Reference**: Psalm 14:3
**Text snippet**: "They have all turned aside; together they have become corrupt; there is none that does good, not even one"
**Challenge type**: Corporate guilt statement - "they/none" excludes God, but uses collective language about human condition
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Corporate confession without direct prayer language - tests if rule applies to guilt statements
**Challenge category**: Tests scope of Rule 2.1 beyond explicit prayers

---

### 2. Psalm 25:1-5
**Reference**: Psalm 25:2
**Text snippet**: "O LORD...In you do I trust...Cause me to know your ways, O LORD; teach me your paths"
**Challenge type**: Individual prayer with appeal to God - "your ways, your paths" (God's possessions, not included in "we")
**Expected difficulty**: Easy
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Direct prayer using possessive adjectives - Rule 2.1 foundation case
**Challenge category**: Possession language in prayer

---

### 3. Psalm 33:1-3
**Reference**: Psalm 33:3
**Text snippet**: "Sing to him a new song; play skillfully with a loud shout"
**Challenge type**: Community worship command - implied "we" in community context
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Community action in worship - does shared action make "we" inclusive or exclusive of God?
**Challenge category**: Tests if worship contexts override standard rules

---

### 4. Psalm 45:17
**Reference**: Psalm 45:17
**Text snippet**: "I will cause your name to be remembered in all generations; therefore nations will praise you forever and ever"
**Challenge type**: Promise of future corporate praise - "nations will praise" (not first-person but about collective future)
**Expected difficulty**: Hard
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Corporate future prayer/praise - extends Rule 2.1 into future tense
**Challenge category**: Temporal extension of prayer language

---

### 5. Psalm 64:1-2
**Reference**: Psalm 64:1
**Text snippet**: "Hear my voice, O God, in my complaint; preserve my life from dread of the enemy"
**Challenge type**: Individual lament - "my complaint, my life" but addressed to God (not collective but accusatory)
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Individual prayer structure with exclusive meaning
**Challenge category**: Baseline Rule 2.1 case (individual version)

---

### 6. Psalm 79:1-4
**Reference**: Psalm 79:4
**Text snippet**: "We have become a taunt to our neighbors, mocked and derided by those around us"
**Challenge type**: National lament - "we...our" corporate identity in distress, prayer to God about external mockery
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Corporate suffering prayer - "we" is collective humans, addressed to God
**Challenge category**: Corporate lament with "we/our" structure

---

### 7. Psalm 80:1-8
**Reference**: Psalm 80:3
**Text snippet**: "Restore us, O God; let your face shine, that we may be saved"
**Challenge type**: Prayer for national restoration - "us...we" (Israel) praying to God for salvation
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: "We may be saved" = people's conditional status, excludes God
**Challenge category**: Conditional "we" in prayer

---

### 8. Psalm 85:1-7
**Reference**: Psalm 85:6
**Text snippet**: "Will you not revive us again, that your people may rejoice in you?"
**Challenge type**: Community restoration prayer - "us...your people" - God's possession language in prayer context
**Expected difficulty**: Hard
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Possessive "your people" mirrors "our God" - both exclude speaker's group membership
**Challenge category**: Possessive language with group identity

---

### 9. Psalm 89:1-4
**Reference**: Psalm 89:4
**Text snippet**: "I will establish your line forever and make your throne firm through all generations"
**Challenge type**: Covenant affirmation - singular speaker promises corporate future
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: First-person singular making plural promise - tests Rule 2.1 scope
**Challenge category**: Singular speaker, plural referent

---

### 10. Psalm 106:1-6
**Reference**: Psalm 106:6
**Text snippet**: "Both we and our ancestors have sinned; we have committed iniquity and done wickedness"
**Challenge type**: Corporate confession spanning generations - "we and our ancestors" (temporal extension of "we")
**Expected difficulty**: Hard
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: "Our ancestors" in "we" group creates temporal ambiguity
**Challenge category**: Historical "we" in confession

## PROPHETS (10 verses)

### 11. Jeremiah 14:7-9
**Reference**: Jeremiah 14:7
**Text snippet**: "Though our iniquities testify against us, act, O LORD, for your name's sake; for our backslidings are many, we have sinned against you"
**Challenge type**: Prophetic intercession - Prophet speaking for people, double "our" + confession to God
**Expected difficulty**: Hard
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: "Our iniquities...we have sinned" - prophet's "we" represents Israel speaking to God
**Challenge category**: Proxy prayer with group identity

---

### 12. Jeremiah 31:31-34
**Reference**: Jeremiah 31:33
**Text snippet**: "I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts; and I will be their God, and they shall be my people"
**Challenge type**: New covenant promise - God speaking about future relationship
**Expected difficulty**: Easy
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: God claims relationship ("I will be their God") - not a prayer but theological statement about group
**Challenge category**: Divine speech claiming group relationship

---

### 13. Isaiah 26:12-19
**Reference**: Isaiah 26:13
**Text snippet**: "O LORD our God, other lords besides you have ruled over us, but your name alone do we acknowledge"
**Challenge type**: Isaiah's prayer - "our God...we acknowledge" - prayer with double exclusive structure
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: "Our God" in prayer directly tests Rule 2.1
**Challenge category**: Rule 2.1 foundation case in prophecy

---

### 14. Isaiah 33:2
**Reference**: Isaiah 33:2
**Text snippet**: "O LORD, be gracious to us; we wait for you. Be our arm every morning, our salvation in the time of trouble"
**Challenge type**: Brief prayer for aid - "us...we...our" triple structure in plea
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Repeated "our" in possession language tests Rule 2.1 consistency
**Challenge category**: Multiple possessive structures in single verse

---

### 15. Isaiah 40:25-31
**Reference**: Isaiah 40:31
**Text snippet**: "But they who wait for the LORD shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles; they shall run and not be weary; they shall walk and not faint"
**Challenge type**: Prophetic promise about "those who wait" - collective future promise
**Expected difficulty**: Medium
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: Corporate promise about God's people - tests Rule 2.1 in promise context
**Challenge category**: Future collective action language

---

### 16. Ezekiel 20:27-29
**Reference**: Ezekiel 20:27
**Text snippet**: "Therefore, mortal, speak to the house of Israel and say to them, Thus says the Lord GOD: In this too your ancestors showed their treachery, for they were unfaithful to me"
**Challenge type**: God's accusation about corporate ancestor sin - "your ancestors...they...me"
**Expected difficulty**: Hard
**Why Rule 2.1 relevant**: God distinguishes between "your" group and "me" - tests Rule 2.1 in divine judgment
**Challenge category**: Divine speech with group accusation

### 17. Hosea 14:2-3
**Reference**: Hosea 14:2
**Text snippet**: "Return, O Israel, to the LORD your God, for you have stumbled because of your iniquity. Take with you words and return to the LORD; say to him, 'Take away all guilt; accept that which is good, and we will offer the fruit of our lips'"

**Expected difficulty**: Hard

**Challenge category**: Conditional future prayer

### 18. Micah 7:14-20

**Challenge category**: Possessive petition structure

## CHALLENGE CATEGORIES

### Rule 2.1 Tests (Direct Prayer to God)
- Verses 2, 13, 24: "Our God" / "Your God" in prayer
- Verses 7, 8: Future salvation contingent on prayer
- Verses 11, 17, 20, 23: Corporate confession/repentance to God

### Corporate Identity Tests
- Verses 6, 7, 16, 19: "We/our" representing collective Israel/community
- Verses 21, 22: Lament spanning individual and corporate

### Proxy/Mediation Tests
- Verses 11, 20: Prophet/leader speaking "we" for community to God
- Verse 25: Non-prayer petition (Satan to God about Job)

### Temporal Extension Tests
- Verses 5, 10, 15, 18: Future tense or historical "we"

### Possession Language Tests
- Verses 8, 14, 18, 24: "Your people," "your ways," "your heritage" in prayers
